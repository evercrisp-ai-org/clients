## Layer 1: Static Annual Cycles

Recurring financial events and deadlines that create natural content windows for orthopedic surgeons. These are predictable and can be planned months in advance. Content should lead these dates by 4-6 weeks (the planning window) rather than publishing on the deadline itself.

### January

**Financial events:**
- New retirement contribution limits take effect (401k, IRA, HSA, cash balance plan, defined benefit)
- New tax brackets and standard deduction amounts active
- New estate and gift tax exemption amounts active
- Q4 estimated tax paid (prior year, Jan 15)

**Content windows:**
- "New year, new limits" — what changed and what to do about it
- Annual financial review and goal-setting for the practice
- Q4 practice performance debrief
- Cash balance plan contribution strategy for the new year

**Surgeon context:** Start of the new fiscal year for many practices. Surgeons are (briefly) more open to strategic conversations after the holiday break.

---

### February

**Financial events:**
- Tax documents arriving (W-2s, 1099s, K-1s, 1098s)
- Last day for prior-year IRA contributions (April 15, but planning starts now)
- Charitable giving receipts finalized for prior year

**Content windows:**
- "What your tax documents are actually telling you" — reading between the lines
- Prior-year IRA contribution reminder (backdoor Roth window)
- Practice P&L analysis — is the entity structure still optimal?
- Tax prep checklist for surgeon practice owners

**Surgeon context:** Tax document season. Surgeons are receiving materials but many default to handing everything to their CPA. Content should frame this as a strategic moment, not an administrative one.

---

### March

**Financial events:**
- Q1 coming to a close — first quarter practice revenue picture emerging
- S-Corp reasonable compensation review window
- Medicare/Medicaid reimbursement rate changes (if applicable)

**Content windows:**
- Q1 financial health check
- S-Corp salary optimization — are you paying yourself the right amount?
- Mid-Q1 tax planning moves (before April 15)
- Practice overhead benchmarking

**Surgeon context:** Full surgical schedules. Content must be concise and high-impact. Respect that March is one of the busiest OR months.

---

### April

**Financial events:**
- April 15: Tax filing deadline and Q1 estimated tax payment
- Prior-year IRA contribution deadline
- HSA contribution deadline for prior year

**Content windows:**
- "What your return just told you" — post-filing insights and action items
- Extension filing strategy — when it makes sense (and when it doesn't)
- Estimated tax payment strategy for the year ahead
- The 6-month extension: what it does and does not do (deadline moves to October 15)

**Surgeon context:** Many surgeons file extensions. Content should normalize this and frame it as strategic, not procrastination.

---

### May

**Financial events:**
- Q2 underway — mid-year planning window opens
- Medical conference season begins (AAOS, regional meetings)
- Practice valuation cycle often begins (for those considering transition in 12-24 months)

**Content windows:**
- Mid-year financial review framework
- Practice valuation — when to get one and why
- Conference networking and its financial implications (practice acquisition, partnership changes)
- The 5-year exit timeline — what to start doing now

**Surgeon context:** Conference season creates natural conversation openers. Surgeons are exposed to practice transition ideas at industry events. Capitalize on that priming.

---

### June

**Financial events:**
- Q2 estimated tax payment (June 15)
- Mid-year investment portfolio review
- Open enrollment planning begins for practice employees

**Content windows:**
- Mid-year tax check — are estimated payments on track?
- Portfolio rebalancing and tax-loss harvesting opportunities
- Practice benefits review — are you maximizing deductions through employee benefits?
- Personal goodwill planning (for future practice sales)

**Surgeon context:** Summer slowdown begins for some surgical specialties. Slightly more bandwidth for financial planning conversations.

---

### July

**Financial events:**
- Mid-year mark — half the year's income is earned
- Practice financial mid-year review
- Second half tax strategy window opens

**Content windows:**
- "Halftime report" — where do you stand against your annual plan?
- Cash balance plan contribution pacing — are you on track?
- Practice transition planning horizons (for those 3-5 years out)
- Income acceleration vs. deferral strategy for the remaining year

**Surgeon context:** Mid-year is a natural reflection point. Content should provide a structured "halftime" framework — diagnosis, assessment, adjustment.

---

### August

**Financial events:**
- Year-end tax strategy positioning begins (the real planning window)
- Open enrollment preparation for practice and personal insurance
- Practice succession planning season

**Content windows:**
- Year-end tax strategy preview — what moves to make before December
- Open enrollment guide for practice owners (not employees)
- Succession planning deep dive — what does a 3-year runway look like?
- Charitable giving strategy for the remaining year (DAFs, bunching)

**Surgeon context:** Return from summer. Increased receptivity to "get ahead of year-end" messaging. This is the true start of the year-end planning window — not November.

---

### September

**Financial events:**
- Q3 estimated tax payment (September 15)
- Extended filing deadline approaching (October 15)
- Estate planning awareness month
- Year-end financial planning intensifies

**Content windows:**
- Estate planning urgency — the exemption sunset and what it means
- Extended filing deadline — last-minute strategies
- Q3 estimated payment check-in
- Roth conversion window analysis (before year-end)

**Surgeon context:** Estate planning is particularly relevant — surgeons in this age range often have estates that could be affected by exemption changes. The sunset provision creates genuine urgency (not manufactured).

---

### October

**Financial events:**
- October 15: Extended filing deadline
- Q4 tax optimization window is open
- Estate planning crunch (for those triggered by September awareness)
- Annual benefits open enrollment period

**Content windows:**
- Post-extension filing debrief — what to do differently next year
- Q4 tax moves — the final optimization window
- Tax-loss harvesting execution
- Retirement plan contribution catch-up (for those behind on cash balance or 401k)

**Surgeon context:** The October 15 deadline creates a natural reset moment. Content should position Q4 as the "final quarter" of the financial year — time to execute, not plan.

---

### November

**Financial events:**
- Year-end charitable giving window
- Final window for tax-loss harvesting
- Required Minimum Distribution deadline approaching (December 31)
- Practice year-end financials being prepared

**Content windows:**
- Strategic charitable giving — DAFs, bunching, appreciated securities
- The RMD checklist (for those subject to distributions)
- Practice year-end financial review
- Year-end retirement contribution planning

**Surgeon context:** Holiday season begins. Attention is split. Content should be actionable and checklist-oriented — "do these three things before December 31."

---

### December

**Financial events:**
- December 31: Year-end tax deadline for most strategies
- Final retirement contributions (401k, cash balance plan)
- Required Minimum Distributions due
- Charitable giving deadline
- Annual review and next-year goal-setting

**Content windows:**
- "Before midnight December 31" — the final checklist
- Annual financial review framework
- Goal-setting for the new year
- Reflective/philosophical content — what did this year teach you about wealth?

**Surgeon context:** Lowest bandwidth month. Content should be extremely concise or reflective/philosophical (matching the end-of-year mood). Checklists outperform essays in December.

## Layer 2: Dynamic Research Checkpoints

A structured process for scanning the environment at set intervals. Each scan produces a brief update noting what changed, what content it affects, and recommended action.

### Monthly Scan

**Run by the 5th of each month. Duration: 1-2 hours.**

Check the following:
- [ ] Federal Reserve decisions (rate changes, forward guidance)
- [ ] Major market movements (S&P 500, bond yields, significant sector moves)
- [ ] New legislation introduced or passed affecting: taxes, retirement, healthcare, estate planning
- [ ] IRS guidance, rulings, or notices relevant to high-income taxpayers
- [ ] CMS updates affecting physician reimbursement
- [ ] Orthopedic industry news (AAOS announcements, consolidation activity, PE deals)

**Output format:**

```
## Monthly Relevance Scan — [Month Year]

### What Changed
- [Item 1: brief description + source]
- [Item 2: brief description + source]

### Content Impact
- [Existing content affected: title + recommended action (proceed/revise/hold)]
- [New content opportunity: brief description + suggested timing]

### Recommended Actions
- [Action 1]
- [Action 2]
```

### Quarterly Deep Scan

**Run in the first week of January, April, July, October. Duration: 3-4 hours.**

Everything in the Monthly Scan, plus:
- [ ] Comprehensive IRS update review (new regulations, proposed rules, final rules)
- [ ] CMS reimbursement changes and their impact on orthopedic practice economics
- [ ] Practice valuation trend analysis (multiples, deal activity)
- [ ] Estate and gift tax developments (legislative, regulatory, judicial)
- [ ] Orthopedic industry trend report (workforce, technology, practice models)
- [ ] Competitive scan: what are other advisors targeting surgeons publishing?
- [ ] Review of content performance data (feeds into Recursive Learning Loop)

**Output format:** Same as Monthly Scan but expanded with a "Quarterly Themes" section identifying 2-3 macro themes for the upcoming quarter.

### Event-Triggered Scan

**Activated immediately when a major event occurs. Duration: 1-2 hours.**

Trigger conditions:
- New tax law signed or major tax bill advances through committee
- Fed makes an unexpected rate decision
- Significant market disruption (>5% index move in a week)
- Major healthcare policy change affecting physicians
- Supreme Court decision affecting taxes, estate planning, or healthcare
- AAOS or major orthopedic organization issues significant guidance

**Output format:**

```
## Event-Triggered Scan — [Date]

### Event
[What happened, when, source]

### Immediate Content Impact
- [Content currently in production: impact assessment]
- [Scheduled content: proceed/revise/hold/pull]

### Rapid Response Opportunity
- [If timely content should be created: topic, angle, urgency level, target channel]

### Longer-Term Implications
- [How this affects the editorial plan beyond the immediate response]
```

## Layer 3: Signal-Driven Content Triggers

Opportunities that emerge from the environment rather than the calendar. These cannot be pre-scheduled but can be recognized by monitoring specific signal categories.

### Legislative Signals

- New bill introduced affecting physician taxes, retirement plans, practice structure, or estate planning
- Tax reform proposals that change brackets, deductions, or exemptions
- Healthcare legislation affecting practice economics or physician compensation
- State-level tax changes in states with high surgeon concentrations

**Response:** Enters the content workflow through the Research Block with elevated priority. Produces a timely analysis piece. Still passes through quality gates and voice validation.

### Market Signals

- Significant market events affecting surgeon portfolios (bear market, sector rotation, rate shock)
- Changes in practice valuation multiples or deal activity
- Interest rate shifts affecting practice debt, mortgages, or fixed-income allocations
- Real estate market changes affecting surgeon property portfolios

**Response:** Context-setting content that helps surgeons understand the implications without panic. Mirrors Jared's "cautionary but not fear-based" tone.

### Industry Signals

- Private equity activity in orthopedics (acquisitions, roll-ups)
- Consolidation trends affecting independent practice viability
- Reimbursement shifts (CMS, commercial payer renegotiations)
- Technology changes affecting surgical practice (robotics, AI in diagnostics)
- Workforce trends (physician shortage, burnout data, retirement patterns)

**Response:** Thought leadership content positioning Capable Wealth as understanding the surgeon's full context, not just their finances.

### Cultural Signals

- Mainstream media coverage of physician burnout, retirement, wealth
- High-profile stories about physician financial mistakes or successes
- Broader cultural moments around wealth, taxes, or healthcare that the audience is already discussing
- Social media trends in physician communities (#MedTwitter, physician finance forums)

**Response:** Content that meets the audience where the conversation already is. Jared's voice is naturally suited to this — he often riffs on things he has seen or heard.

### Competitive Signals

- What other advisors targeting surgeons are publishing
- Gaps in competitor content that Capable Wealth can fill
- Topics competitors are overemphasizing (opportunity to differentiate)
- New entrants in the surgeon financial advisory space

**Response:** Strategic positioning. Not reactive to competitors, but aware of the landscape. Lead where others follow.

## Layer 4: Research Source Library

Standing sources for each scan type. These are starting points, not exhaustive lists.

### Tax and Legislation
- IRS.gov (Newsroom, Revenue Procedures, Notices)
- Congressional Research Service reports
- Tax Foundation analysis
- Journal of Accountancy
- Major accounting firm tax alerts (PwC, Deloitte, EY, KPMG)

### Market and Investment
- Federal Reserve economic data (FRED)
- Bureau of Labor Statistics
- S&P Global Market Intelligence
- Morningstar
- Wall Street Journal Markets section

### Orthopedic Industry
- AAOS Now (American Academy of Orthopaedic Surgeons)
- Becker's Spine Review / ASC Review
- Medscape Orthopedics Compensation Report
- Medical Group Management Association (MGMA) data
- Healthcare private equity trackers (PitchBook, Bain healthcare reports)

### Estate and Retirement
- IRS retirement plan contribution limit announcements (annual, typically October/November)
- Estate tax exemption updates
- Social Security Administration announcements
- Department of Labor retirement plan guidance

### Practice Economics
- MGMA physician compensation data
- AAOS Practice Management Committee publications
- Medical Economics magazine
- Advisory Board physician practice research
