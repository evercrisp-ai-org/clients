# Experience Interview Guide

> This document captures the real context behind [PERSON_NAME] and [ORG_NAME]. Its purpose is to give the content system an accurate foundation so that every piece of content is true, compliant, and resonant. Answer each question honestly. Short answers are fine. "Not yet" and "N/A" are valid.

**How to use this guide:**
- Work through each section at your own pace. Some sections may take reflection.
- After completing it, the responses will be synthesized into [experience-inventory.md](experience-inventory.md), which the AI references during content creation.
- Review and update this document at least quarterly, or whenever something material changes.

**Companion documents:**
- [experience-inventory.md](experience-inventory.md): The structured reference document populated from these responses
- [voice-profile.md](voice-profile.md): WHO [PERSON_NAME] is as a communicator
- [content-recipe.md](content-recipe.md): HOW content gets produced, including the Content Integrity Filter

## Section 1: Professional Timeline

These questions establish verifiable facts about the career. Content can reference these directly.

1. When did you earn your primary credential or designation? (Month/year)

2. How many total years have you worked in your industry?

3. What firms or roles did you hold before launching [ORG_NAME]? (Role descriptions and durations.)

4. When did [ORG_NAME] officially launch? (Month/year)

5. What licenses, registrations, or designations do you currently hold?

6. Are there any professional milestones or recognitions worth noting?

---

## Section 2: Current Client Base

These questions define what the content system can honestly say about the practice today.

7. Approximately how many active clients do you currently serve?

8. What industries or professions do your current clients represent?

9. What is the general income or seniority range of your current client base?

10. How long have you been working with your longest-tenured client?

11. What services do you provide most frequently today?

12. Do you work with clients in a team or co-advisory capacity with other professionals?

## Section 3: [AUDIENCE] Experience

These questions determine how the content system handles audience-specific claims. This is where the most fabrication risk exists.

13. Have you worked with any [AUDIENCE] members as clients? If yes, how many and for how long?

14. Have you worked with any professionals in related fields whose situations are similar to [AUDIENCE]? If yes, describe generally.

15. What is your plan for entering or deepening your work with [AUDIENCE]?

16. Have you attended any industry events, conferences, or networking groups relevant to [AUDIENCE]?

17. Do you have any personal connections to the [AUDIENCE] community?

## Section 5: Knowledge Areas

Rate your competency in each area relevant to your content. Be honest: "studied" is not "practiced."

For each area, mark:
- **Practiced:** I have done this with real clients.
- **Studied:** I have deep knowledge but have not applied it in practice yet.
- **Familiar:** I understand the basics and could discuss it at a high level.

## Section 6: Personal Anecdotes

These are stories from your own life that can be used in content without any client privacy concerns.

18. What are 3-5 personal stories or experiences that shaped your professional philosophy?

19. Are there any personal stories you have already used in published content that should continue to be available?

20. Are there any personal stories that are off-limits?

---

## Section 7: Aspirational Positioning

21. How do you want [ORG_NAME] to be positioned in the market? (Not where you are today, but where you are heading.)

22. What would you like to be known for in 2-3 years?

23. Are there any claims about future positioning that are already being made in content? If so, are they still accurate?

---

## Section 8: Off-Limits and Compliance

24. Are there any topics you must not write about? (Legal restrictions, ethical boundaries, competitive sensitivities.)

25. Are there any claims you must not make? (Regulatory restrictions, unsubstantiated outcomes, comparisons.)

26. Are there any required disclaimers or disclosures for your industry?

27. Is there anything else the content system should know about what it can and cannot say on your behalf?
