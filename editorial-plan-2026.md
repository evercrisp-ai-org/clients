# Editorial Plan: March 2026 – February 2027

> Capable Wealth — Precision planning for life beyond the OR.

This 12-month editorial plan is built on the static annual cycles from the Content Relevance Calendar, filtered through Jared's voice and philosophy, and grounded in current 2026 financial conditions. Every piece is designed for orthopedic surgeons ages 45–65 who are approaching practice transition or retirement.

---

## Current Landscape Snapshot (as of February 2026)

**Tax Environment**
- The One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA), signed July 4, 2025, made TCJA individual tax provisions permanent. The 37% top rate is here to stay.
- 2026 top bracket: 37% on taxable income above $640,600 (single) / $768,600 (MFJ)
- Standard deduction: $12,400 (single) / $27,750 (MFJ)

**Retirement Contribution Limits (2026)**
- 401(k) elective deferral: $24,500 (catch-up 50+: $8,000; super catch-up 60–63: $11,250)
- IRA: $7,500 (catch-up 50+: $1,100)
- Defined contribution total: $72,000
- Defined benefit annual benefit: $290,000
- HSA: $4,400 individual / $8,750 family (catch-up 55+: $1,000)
- Annual compensation limit: $360,000

**Estate & Gift Tax**
- OBBBA permanently increased the estate/gift/GST exemption to $15 million per individual / $30 million per couple (2026, indexed for inflation)
- No sunset provision — the doubled exemption is now permanent
- Annual gift tax exclusion: $19,000

**Federal Reserve**
- Current fed funds rate: 3.50%–3.75% (held steady at January 28, 2026 FOMC meeting)
- Two FOMC dissenters preferred a quarter-point cut
- Market expects at least one additional cut in 2026, potentially by summer
- Powell: "Hard to look at the data and say policy is significantly restrictive right now"

**Medicare & Reimbursement**
- CMS 2026 Physician Fee Schedule includes a 2.5% "efficiency adjustment" cutting payments for non-time-based specialty procedures, directly impacting orthopedic surgeries
- OBBBA ties Medicare physician payments to 75% of the Medicare Economic Index (MEI) in 2026, dropping to 10% of MEI annually thereafter
- Physician organizations warn this "will provide little relief from the inflationary pressures that practices continue to face"

**Orthopedic Practice Economics**
- Median orthopedic surgeon compensation: ~$800,000
- PE consolidation in its "second wave" — major 2024–2025 deals include Revelstoke/UnitedHealth (Ortho Alliance), Brookfield/Varsity (Orthopedic Care Partners), and WCAS/Constitution Surgery Alliance
- Procedure volume growth: knee replacements projected +673% by 2030; hip replacements +174%
- Accelerating shift from inpatient to outpatient/ASC settings
- Hospital/system-owned practices now outperforming private practices on median work RVUs — a notable reversal

## Month 1: March 2026

### Theme: Q1 Financial Health Check — Are You Paying Yourself the Right Amount?

**Surgeon context:** Full surgical schedules — March is one of the busiest OR months. Content must be concise and high-impact. Surgeons are heads-down on patient care, but Q1 revenue data is emerging and the April 15 deadline looms.

**Content 1: "You're Not Overtaxed. You're Understructured."**

Walk through the real cost of a misaligned S-Corp salary, an underfunded cash balance plan, and a missing cost segregation study. - **Primary channel:** Blog

**Content 2: "The S-Corp Salary Trap: Why Your CPA's 'Reasonable Compensation' Might Be Costing You"**

- **Target angle:** S-Corp reasonable compensation review window opens in Q1. Most surgeon-owners default to whatever their CPA set years ago. Challenge the assumption that "reasonable" means "optimal." Show how the right salary calibration balances self-employment tax savings against retirement plan contribution capacity — and how getting it wrong in either direction costs real money at $745K+ in income.
- **Theme mapping:** Tax structure optimization
- **Primary channel:** LinkedIn

**Content 3: "The 2.5% Cut Nobody's Talking About — and What It Actually Means for Your Practice"**

- **Target angle:** CMS's 2026 "efficiency adjustment" cuts reimbursement on non-time-based orthopedic procedures by 2.5%. Most surgeons will hear about this from their practice manager, not their financial advisor. **Surgeon context:** Tax documents are arriving (W-2s, 1099s, K-1s, 1098s). Many surgeons default to handing everything to their CPA without review. Content should frame this as a strategic moment, not an administrative one. Prior-year IRA contribution window is open (deadline April 15).

Each document tells a story about the prior year's structure — and hints at what needs to change for the current year.
- **Theme mapping:** Tax structure optimization
- **Primary channel:** Blog

- **Target angle:** February, when all prior-year financial data is in hand, is the ideal time to evaluate whether the practice's entity structure still makes sense. S-Corp vs. C-Corp vs. partnership, the interplay between entity type and retirement plan design, and whether recent changes (OBBBA provisions, state tax law shifts, practice growth or contraction) warrant a restructuring. Most surgeons set their entity structure once and never revisit it. That inertia is expensive.
- **Theme mapping:** Tax structure optimization + Practice revenue optimization
- **Primary channel:** Blog

- **Target angle:** Annual reminder that the prior-year IRA contribution window is open until April 15. But rather than a generic "don't forget to fund your IRA" piece, use this as a decision framework: should you fund a backdoor Roth, fund a traditional IRA, or redirect those dollars to a more impactful strategy (cash balance plan increase, HSA max-out, DAF contribution)? - **Theme mapping:** Cash balance plan compounding + Tax structure optimization
- **Primary channel:** LinkedIn

- **Target angle:** The closing piece for the editorial year. Look back at the 12-month arc: from the Q1 health check through the year-end execution to this moment of new-year clarity. - **Theme mapping:** Life beyond the OR
- **Primary channel:** YouTube
