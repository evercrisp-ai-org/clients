13 points of rate spread is the whole game in income timing.

Every accelerate-or-defer decision reduces to one comparison: this year's top rate against next year's projected top rate. The dollars matter far less than the gap between those two numbers.

Here is the mechanism, in three steps.

First, project next year's top marginal rate. Not your average rate, the rate on your last dollars, after a likely exit, a step-back, or a state move.

Second, set it next to this year's. A surgeon at the 37% bracket who expects a quiet 24% year next year is staring at a 13-point spread. That spread is the entire opportunity.

Third, move income toward the lower-rate year. Lower next year, lean toward deferral. Higher next year, accelerate income into this year while the lower brackets are still open.

When the two years are equal, the spread is zero, and timing does nothing but tie up cash. That is the case to stop running on reflex.

The math is not complicated. The discipline is in projecting next year honestly before you move a dollar.

What is your projected top rate next year, and how does it compare to this one?
