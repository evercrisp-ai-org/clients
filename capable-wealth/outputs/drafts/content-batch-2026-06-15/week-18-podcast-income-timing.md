## COLD OPEN (30-60 seconds)

Deferring income is a bet on next year's tax rate. For most of the last decade, that was close to a free bet. This year it is not, and a lot of surgeons are still placing it out of habit.

Today we take a decision that sounds like accounting housekeeping and turn it into a clear second-half choice: do you pull income into this year, push it into next year, or leave it alone? The answer is worth six figures for some of you, and the deadline to decide well is closer than December.

---
## INTRO AND CONTEXT (1-2 minutes)

Here is why this matters right now. We are near the midpoint of the year. You can see your run rate. You know whether the practice is tracking ahead of last year, and you have a rough sense of what 2027 looks like. That is exactly the information you need to make a timing decision, and it is exactly the information you will not have in a useful form if you wait until the fourth quarter.

The One Big Beautiful Bill Act made the 37% top rate permanent. That single fact rewires the old playbook, because the reflex to defer income assumed rates were temporary and would fall. They are not, and they will not on their own. So every deferral now needs a real reason.

---
## SEGMENT 1: Deferral Is a Bet on Next Year's Rate (3-5 minutes)

`[CLIP-CANDIDATE]`

When you defer income, you are wagering that the dollar you move into next year gets taxed at a lower rate than it would face this year. When that is true, it is one of the cleanest moves in planning. When it is false, you have delayed a bill and added a year of uncertainty for nothing.

For a surgeon whose 2026 and 2027 look about the same, the marginal rate does not move. The top dollar is taxed at 37% in both years. You shuffled the calendar and kept the identical result, minus the cash you tied up waiting. That is the version of deferral I want you to stop doing on autopilot.

---
## SEGMENT 2: The Four Variables That Decide It (3-5 minutes)

`[CLIP-CANDIDATE]`

Four questions tell you which direction to lean.

First, is a practice exit on the calendar? A sale year stacks ordinary income and gains together, which usually argues for pulling income forward into a lighter year.

Second, is there a genuine low-income gap year ahead, a sabbatical or a wind-down? That is the rare, real case for deferral.

Third, are you changing states? Moving from a high-tax state to a no-tax state changes the value of every deferred dollar.

Fourth, how is your retirement funding paced? Income timing feeds the W-2 compensation that drives 401(k) and cash balance contributions, so the decisions are linked.

---
## SEGMENT 3: Same $200K, Three Right Answers (3-5 minutes)

`[CLIP-CANDIDATE]`

Picture a surgeon projecting $850,000 this year, deciding what to do with $200,000 of distributions.

If next year looks like this year, the money is taxed at 37% either way, so deferral saves nothing. Take it now and put it to work.

If the surgeon is stepping back next year, with income closer to $300,000 and top dollars near the 24% bracket, deferring that $200,000 saves roughly $26,000. That is the deferral that earns its keep.

If the surgeon is selling next year, next year is the heavy year. Now you accelerate income into this year to use the lower brackets before the sale fills them. Same surgeon, same $200,000, three different answers. The dollar amount never changed. The calendar decided everything.

---
## SEGMENT 4: The Conversation to Have Before July (3-5 minutes)

The whole decision rests on one question: is 2027 higher, lower, or the same as 2026? If you cannot answer that with reasonable confidence, you are not ready to move income in either direction, and forcing it is how a timing decision becomes a mistake instead of a save.

So the late-June conversation is short and specific. Lay out the projection for both years. Name the events that change it, an exit, a move, a step back. Then decide the direction and assign the levers: payroll, distribution timing, retirement funding. Decide now, while all of those levers still move easily.

---
## LISTENER TAKEAWAY (1-2 minutes)

One action this week: write down your best estimate of 2027 income next to your 2026 projection. If they are roughly equal, stop deferring on reflex. If next year is clearly lighter or clearly heavier, that gap is your signal, and it is worth a focused call with your advisor before the third quarter starts.

---
## PHILOSOPHICAL CLOSE (1-2 minutes)

The point of all of this is not to win a tax game. It is control. When you know which direction your income should move and why, you stop reacting to deadlines and start deciding on your own terms.

That is what turns a high income into real wealth. The number on your tax return matters less than whether you chose it deliberately. Precision in timing, like precision in the OR, is mostly the discipline of asking the sharper question before the moment forces a worse one.
