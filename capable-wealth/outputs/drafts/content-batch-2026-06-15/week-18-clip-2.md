## Content

### Caption Script

Same surgeon. Same $200,000 of income. Three different right answers.

Picture a surgeon projecting $850,000 this year, deciding what to do with $200,000 of distributions.

If next year looks the same, that money is taxed at 37% either way, so deferral saves nothing.

If the surgeon is stepping back next year, with income near $300,000, deferring into that lighter year saves about $26,000.

If the surgeon is selling next year, you do the opposite. You pull income into this year, before the sale fills the lower brackets.

The dollar amount never changed. The calendar decided everything.

So the real question is simple: what does your next year actually look like?
