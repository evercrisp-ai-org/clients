Two surgeons. Same practice sale. Same net proceeds: $2.9 million after taxes and closing costs. Here's where they stood three years later.

The unplanned path: lifestyle expanded by 30% in year one. A $500,000 speculative investment went in without formal due diligence. Another $200,000 in drawdowns in year two with no budget framework. By year three, $1.7 million remained. A 41% decline.

The planned path: a structured annual budget of $375,000, agreed upon before the sale. A diversified investment plan designed before the money arrived. A 90-day waiting period for any purchase or investment over $100,000. By year three, $3.2 million. A 10% increase.

Same starting point. $1.5 million apart.

The difference was not investment genius. The surgeon with the plan didn't pick better stocks or time any market. She put three structures in place: a budget that matched her values (generous, intentional), an investment framework that removed emotion from deployment, and a waiting period that filtered impulsive decisions.

Those three structures cost nothing to build and protected $1.5 million in three years.

If you're anywhere in the practice transition conversation, the most valuable thing you can build today is a next-chapter plan. The math makes the case on its own.
