$1.6 million of a $4 million practice sale could be taxed at 23.8% instead of 32% or higher. The variable is your reputation.

Consider a surgeon who has spent 25 years building referral relationships with primary care physicians and sports medicine colleagues across a metro area. Sixty percent of new patients arrive through those personal relationships. They come because of the surgeon's name, clinical results, and professional standing. If the surgeon left tomorrow, those referrals would follow.

That's personal goodwill. It has a measurable dollar value. And when properly documented, it receives capital gains tax treatment instead of ordinary income treatment.

Here's the part most surgeons miss: the documentation must be in place before any buyer conversations begin. A non-compete agreement signed the same week as a purchase agreement signals to the IRS that the goodwill claim was manufactured for the deal.

The documentation timeline is 12 to 24 months. Non-compete agreement between you and your practice entity. Systematic tracking of referral sources over at least two years. A formal appraisal that separates personal from enterprise goodwill.

Your surgical reputation, your referral network, your patient loyalty. They have a dollar value. Document it while you still can.
