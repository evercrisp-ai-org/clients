If you're within five years of a potential practice transition, here are three things worth putting in motion:

1. A non-compete agreement between you and your practice entity. This establishes that your individual value to the practice is real and documented. Best to have this in place at least two years before any sale conversations.

2. Referral source tracking. Where do your patients actually come from? How many arrive through your personal relationships versus the practice's institutional channels? You need at least 24 months of data to establish a credible pattern.

3. A personal goodwill appraisal. A formal valuation that separates the value of "you" from the value of "the business." This should be completed at least 18 months before any anticipated sale.

None of these require a buyer to be identified. All can be done alongside your normal practice operations. And the potential tax savings are measured in hundreds of thousands of dollars.
