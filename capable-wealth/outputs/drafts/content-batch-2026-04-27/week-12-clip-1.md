## Content

### Caption Script

Your practice is worth $4.4 million. Or is it $5.5 million? The answer depends on one concept most surgeons have never heard of: normalized EBITDA.

Consider a practice with reported net income of $1.1 million. A straightforward calculation: $1.1 million times a 4x multiple equals $4.4 million. That's the number most surgeons carry in their heads.

But a buyer uses a different number. They adjust for five things: above-market owner compensation, family members on payroll at above-market rates, personal expenses running through the business, one-time costs that won't recur, and below-market lease terms.

After those five adjustments, the same practice shows normalized EBITDA of roughly $1.38 million. At the same 4x multiple, the valuation is $5.5 million.

That's a $1.1 million gap. Same practice. Same year of financial data. Same buyer pool. The only difference is which version of the financials the valuation is based on.

Most surgeons discover this gap when a buyer puts a letter of intent on the table. Understanding it now, years before any transaction, is how you turn a surprise into leverage.
