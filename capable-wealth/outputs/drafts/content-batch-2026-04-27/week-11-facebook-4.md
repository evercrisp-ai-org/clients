Here's something most surgeons learn too late about practice transitions: the IRS looks at when your documentation was created, not just what it says.

If your personal goodwill documentation shows up at the same time as the purchase agreement, it doesn't matter how thorough it is. Courts have reduced personal goodwill allocations from 75% to 15% in cases where the timing suggested the documentation was manufactured for the deal.

The principle is straightforward: start early or don't start at all. Two years of documentation before a sale conversation is credible. Two months of documentation created during the sale process is a tax position that's likely to be challenged.

If this feels like something that should have been on your radar earlier, you're not alone. Most surgeons in practice transition conversations hear about personal goodwill for the first time during the deal itself. By then, the most valuable documentation window has passed.
