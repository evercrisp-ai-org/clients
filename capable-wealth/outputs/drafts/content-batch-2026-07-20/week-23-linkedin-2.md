## Content

Two orthopedic surgeons. Same age, same specialty, similar EBITDA.

One of them spent three years documenting her personal goodwill — referral source attribution, patient loyalty analysis, the clear separation of what stays with the practice versus what leaves when she does. She hired an associate in Year 2. She reduced her practice's surgeon-dependence from 95% to 67% before a buyer ever looked at the books.

The other received an unsolicited offer at 56. He'd been meaning to start the succession conversation for two years. The offer looked good on the surface. In due diligence, the buyer's analyst ran the replacement surgeon model and discounted forward revenue by 14%. The personal goodwill allocation — never documented — became a negotiation the attorney tried and failed to win.

The difference in net proceeds on comparable practices: over $1 million.

Personal goodwill documentation is not a closing-table move. It requires a valuation specialist, a paper trail, and enough lead time that the documentation predates the deal by years — not months.

August is when the surgeons who will exit on their terms in 2028 and 2029 start building that record.

The question for this week: is your practice's value inside the practice, or inside you?
