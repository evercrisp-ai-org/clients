## Content

August is the month when a lot of important decisions get made without feeling like decisions.

The summer schedule clears slightly. The fall urgency hasn't arrived yet. There's a window — maybe two or three weeks — where the big, long-horizon questions get some mental space that they don't get the rest of the year.

For orthopedic surgeons in their mid-50s, one of those questions is worth spending some of that window on.

If I'm planning to exit this practice in three to five years, have I actually started?

Not in the abstract — not "I've thought about it" — but actually started. Is a valuation specialist working on the personal goodwill documentation? Has anyone mapped where the practice's referral volume comes from and what percentage would survive without me?

The succession planning conversation doesn't have a natural trigger. There's no referral, no PE firm showing up, no obvious moment to begin. August, when there's a little more space to think, is as good a trigger as any.
