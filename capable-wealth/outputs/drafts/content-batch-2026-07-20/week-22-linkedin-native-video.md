## Video Script / Talking Points

**[ON CAMERA — direct address, conversational, no teleprompter feel]**

**0:00–0:08 (Hook)**
"There are two year-end tax moves that require an August start. Not November. August. And most surgeons don't know either one has a deadline this early."

**0:08–0:25 (Move 1 — DAF)**
"The first is the donor-advised fund. If you have appreciated stock — a single-stock holding, vested RSUs, a mutual fund that has grown since you bought it — and you are planning to give it to charity this year, the account that makes it tax-efficient takes six to eight weeks to set up. Wait until November, and the transfer clears in January. The deduction moves to next year. Start it in August."

**0:25–0:45 (Move 2 — Retirement Plan Amendment)**
"The second is the retirement plan amendment. If you have a cash balance plan and your income has grown significantly since it was established, the plan's contribution formula may be set below what your current age and income allow. Expanding it requires an amendment filed before October 1. That process takes six to eight weeks. Start the conversation with your actuary in August."

**0:45–0:58 (Close)**
"Both of these take ten minutes to initiate. The lead time is the constraint, not the complexity. If either of these applies to your situation, the window is right now."

**[CAPTION for muted viewers]**
"2 year-end tax moves that require August initiation — not November. The DAF setup window and the retirement plan amendment deadline both require 6–8 weeks of lead time. Start now."
