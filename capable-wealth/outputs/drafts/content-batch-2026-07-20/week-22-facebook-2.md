## Content

There are two versions of December for a surgeon practice owner.

In the first version, the phone rings from your CPA on December 15th. You are doing three things at once. You make a decision in fifteen minutes that should have taken three weeks to analyze. You capture about half of what was available.

In the second version, you had the conversation with your advisor in July. The charitable vehicle was established in August. The retirement plan amendment was filed in September. The Roth conversion was modeled and implemented in October. December is quiet.

The difference between those two versions is not strategy. Every well-advised surgeon knows roughly the same set of year-end moves. The difference is when the conversation started.

Summer is the planning window. Not because of any deadline that falls in July — but because the moves that produce the biggest outcomes in December require August initiation. The DAF, the plan amendment, the installment sale structure — they all have longer lead times than people expect.

The surgeons who have a quiet December start in July.
