## Content

$121,600 in combined tax savings from a single transaction. The transaction itself takes 10 minutes to approve. The account that makes it possible takes six to eight weeks to establish.

That is the donor-advised fund math for a surgeon with $200,000 in appreciated stock.

Transferring stock directly to a DAF — rather than selling it and donating cash — avoids capital gains on the appreciation and unlocks a deduction at the full fair market value. For a position with a $30,000 cost basis, the difference between selling first and donating directly is $95,890 in a single year.

The reason most surgeons miss this: they start the conversation in November, when the account processing timeline pushes the transfer into January. The deduction moves to next year.

Major DAF sponsors take four to eight weeks to approve and fund new accounts. The stock transfer itself takes additional time to settle and be acknowledged for the current tax year.

To capture the 2026 deduction, the account needs to be established and funded by October 31 — which means initiating the process in August or September at the latest. The planning conversation starts in July.

If you have an appreciated stock position, a concentrated single-stock holding from a prior bonus, restricted units that have vested, or a mutual fund that has grown substantially since purchase, this is worth modeling before August.

Have you identified your most appreciated position this year?
