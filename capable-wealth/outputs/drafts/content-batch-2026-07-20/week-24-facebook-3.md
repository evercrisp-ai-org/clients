## Content

If you're enrolled in an HDHP and your employer offers a standard healthcare FSA, electing that FSA disqualifies your HSA contribution for every month the FSA is active.

It's the most common HSA disqualifier in physician benefit packages — and it happens by default. The FSA election is often pre-populated or suggested in the enrollment flow. The HSA is an afterthought.

The solution is a limited-purpose FSA, which covers only dental and vision expenses. A limited-purpose FSA does not disqualify HSA eligibility and can be elected in the same plan year as the HSA. For a surgeon who wears glasses and sees a dentist, this is a meaningful additional pre-tax vehicle layered on top of the $9,750 HSA maximum.

The practical checklist: if you're electing the HSA for 2027, confirm that the FSA election you're making is the limited-purpose version, not the standard healthcare FSA. One checkbox on the enrollment form. One distinction that costs $3,608 in annual tax savings when it's missed.
