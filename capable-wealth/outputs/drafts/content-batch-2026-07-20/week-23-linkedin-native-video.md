## Talking Points with Timestamps

**[0:00-0:10] — Hook**
If you're between 50 and 57 and succession planning hasn't started — here's what it costs you to wait. The gap between a three-year planned exit and an 18-month distressed one is approximately $1.5 million in net proceeds, on the same practice.

**[0:10-0:25] — Move 1: Practice Systemization**
Year 3 starts with reducing your practice's surgeon-dependence. Right now, most orthopedic practices run 85 to 95% dependent on the operating surgeon's personal relationships and productivity. A buyer's analyst discounts forward revenue on that basis. Getting that number below 70% before the sale — through formalized scheduling, associate revenue, documented protocols — changes the multiple.

**[0:25-0:40] — Move 2: Personal Goodwill Documentation**
The second Year 3 move is personal goodwill documentation. Personal goodwill — taxed at long-term capital gains rates when properly documented — has to be established before negotiations begin. Once a buyer is at the table, the allocation argument is very hard to win. Start the documentation with a valuation specialist now, and you have a paper trail that predates the deal by years.

**[0:40-0:55] — Close**
For a surgeon planning to exit in 2029, Year 3 is August 2026. The protocol starts this month. The question to ask your advisor: what percentage of your practice's EBITDA is genuinely transferable without you?

**[0:55-1:00] — Diagnostic close**
If you don't know the answer, that's the succession planning conversation you haven't had yet.
