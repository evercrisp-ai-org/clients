## Clip Script

**[COLD START — no intro]**

$121,600 versus $18,570.

Same $200,000 charitable gift. Two completely different tax outcomes.

Here is the difference.

A surgeon has $200,000 in a stock position with a $30,000 cost basis. She wants to give $200,000 to charity this year.

Option one: she sells the stock first, pays capital gains tax — $170,000 in gain at 23.8% is $40,460 — and donates the remaining $159,540. She deducts $159,540 at 37%. That saves $59,030 in income tax, minus the $40,460 she paid in capital gains. Net tax benefit: $18,570.

Option two: she transfers the stock directly to a donor-advised fund. No capital gains triggered. Full $200,000 deduction at 37%. Tax savings: $74,000 in income tax plus $40,460 in avoided capital gains. Total tax benefit: $114,460.

The difference is $95,890. From the same charitable intent.

The reason to start this in July: the DAF account takes six to eight weeks to establish and fund. The stock transfer takes additional time to settle.

Wait until November, and the transfer clears in January. The deduction moves to next year.

The strategy does not change. The timing does. Start it now.
