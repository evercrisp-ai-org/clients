## Content

If you had to sell your practice in 18 months, what would your net proceeds be?

Not the gross valuation. The net number — after tax, after the allocation argument you didn't win, after the multiple discount from a practice that was still 95% dependent on you when the deal closed.

For a surgeon at $900,000 EBITDA, the difference between a 3-year planned exit and an 18-month distressed sale is approximately $1.5 million in net proceeds.

Same practice. Same EBITDA. Same income history.

The difference is when the succession runway started.

The three-year runway has a specific protocol: Year 3 is practice systemization and personal goodwill documentation. Year 2 is the associate hire and referral network mapping. Year 1 is buyer engagement, deal structure, and installment sale analysis.

Most orthopedic practice owners know succession planning matters. What they underestimate is how much of the value is produced in Year 3 — the year furthest from the exit — when there's still time to change the transferability profile of the practice before a buyer's analyst runs the normalization model.

August is the start of that clock for surgeons planning to exit in 2029.

The diagnostic question: if you had to sell your practice in 18 months, would it be ready?
