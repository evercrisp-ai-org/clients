## Content

July is the right time to run the Roth conversion analysis. Most surgeons wait until October or November, when the decision gets rushed and the implementation window shrinks.

Here is the reason July matters: you now have a clear mid-year income picture. You can project your full-year tax liability with reasonable accuracy. You know whether you are tracking ahead or behind your prior estimates. That projection is the foundation of the Roth conversion decision.

With the One Big Beautiful Bill Act making the 37% top rate permanent, the "convert while rates are low" argument needs revisiting. The analysis for a surgeon is no longer simple arithmetic. It involves five variables: your current combined marginal rate (federal plus state), your expected retirement state, Medicare IRMAA thresholds, charitable giving offsets from any DAF contributions, and your RMD trajectory from pre-tax accounts.

A surgical colleague planning to retire in Florida — but currently practicing in New York at a 50%+ combined rate — has a very different Roth conversion calculus than a surgeon who expects to stay in a high-tax state indefinitely.

The analysis takes time. July is when it starts.
