## Content

If you are planning to make any of these five moves before December 31, the clock for two of them is already running.

Year-end tax planning for a surgeon practice owner is not a single event. It is five separate protocols with five different initiation deadlines — and the two that create the most value require the longest lead time.

**The five moves and their deadlines:**

1. Charitable vehicle setup (donor-advised fund): account established and funded by October 31. That means paperwork starts in August.

2. Equipment and Section 179 purchases: items must be placed in service by December 31. Order by September to guarantee delivery and setup before year-end.

3. Retirement plan amendments: structural changes to cash balance and 401(k) plans must be adopted before October 1 for most calendar-year plans.

4. Roth conversion analysis: model the conversion in July and August when the full-year income picture is clear. Implement in October.

5. Installment sale structuring: must be elected before or at the closing of the transaction. No retroactive option.

The mistake is not missing December 31. The mistake is missing August and September, when the most consequential decisions need to be made.

Which of these five is your highest-priority move for 2026?
