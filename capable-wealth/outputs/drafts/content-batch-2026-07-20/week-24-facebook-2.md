## Content

Every November, the same story.

The enrollment packet arrives on a Tuesday. A surgeon has until Friday to submit her elections. She's between two cases, hasn't looked at the benefit summary since last year, and makes the HSA versus FSA decision in about fifteen minutes.

She picks the FSA because she knows what it is. The HSA requires a plan change she doesn't have time to evaluate.

What she didn't know: her current plan's deductible had been increased in the prior year's renewal, and it now qualified as an HDHP. The HDHP-HSA combination was available. The analysis would have taken thirty minutes in August.

The dependent care FSA — her household had $11,000 in qualifying childcare expenses — never got elected at all.

Open enrollment is a tax decision made under time pressure. The surgeons who make it well start the analysis in August, when the enrollment window is still two months out and there's room to think.
