## Content

Quick reflection for a Saturday morning.

Do you know which position in your portfolio has appreciated the most since you bought it?

Not what it is worth today. What it has grown by. The difference between the cost basis and the current value — the gain that will be taxed when you eventually sell.

For many surgeon practice owners, there is a concentrated position from a prior bonus, an RSU vesting event, a mutual fund that has quietly compounded for a decade. That position may be sitting in a taxable account where it is accruing a tax liability every year it grows.

A donor-advised fund allows you to transfer that position directly to a charitable vehicle — avoiding capital gains entirely — while taking a charitable deduction at full market value. The combined tax benefit at surgeon income levels is typically two to three times what a cash donation to the same charity would produce.

The setup window is August and September. The transfer has to settle before year-end.

If you have been meaning to look at this, July is the moment to pull up that account statement and identify the position.
