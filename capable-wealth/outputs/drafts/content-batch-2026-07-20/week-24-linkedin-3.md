## Content

A surgical colleague ran the open enrollment analysis for the first time last August at my prompting. She had been in the same PPO for four years.

The analysis revealed two things: her plan qualified as an HDHP at the $3,300 family deductible threshold, and her household had $8,200 in qualifying dependent care expenses she'd never elected through the dependent care FSA.

Four years of missed dependent care FSA elections: $20,000 in foregone pre-tax deferrals at the $5,000 annual limit, and approximately $7,400 in federal tax savings at the 37% rate.

The four-step open enrollment protocol for orthopedic surgeons:

Step 1: confirm your plan's deductible. Does it meet the HDHP threshold? ($1,650 self-only / $3,300 family for 2026.) If yes, proceed. If no, price the HDHP option and run the net analysis.

Step 2: if HDHP-qualified, confirm you're contributing the maximum HSA. In 2026, that's $9,750 for a surgeon 55 or older.

Step 3: check FSA coordination. A standard healthcare FSA disqualifies HSA eligibility. A limited-purpose FSA (dental and vision only) does not.

Step 4: does your household have qualifying dependent care expenses? Up to $5,000 per household in pre-tax dollars through the dependent care FSA — a separate election from the healthcare FSA, and often overlooked.

The enrollment packet arrives in October. The decision should be made in August.

Which of these four questions can you answer right now?
