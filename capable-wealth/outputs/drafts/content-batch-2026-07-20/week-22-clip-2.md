## Clip Script

**[COLD START]**

$75,000 in unused retirement plan capacity. One amendment. A September 30 deadline that most surgeons don't know exists.

Here is the scenario.

A surgeon established a cash balance plan three years ago. At the time, the plan was designed for $100,000 in annual contributions — conservative, appropriate for where she was. Her income has grown since then. Her plan has room to expand, potentially to $160,000 per year or more based on her current age and compensation.

The expansion requires a plan amendment. A change to the benefit formula, filed with her actuary and plan administrator.

The amendment takes six to eight weeks to complete. Which means the conversation with her actuary needs to happen before September 1 for the amendment to be adopted before October 1.

October 1 is the hard deadline for most calendar-year plans to adopt structural changes that apply to the current plan year.

The math: $60,000 in additional contributions at a 37% marginal rate equals $22,200 in immediate tax savings. Plus the compounding on the additional contributions over time.

If she starts this conversation in October, it is too late for 2026.

If she starts it in July — like right now — it is not.

One question to ask your actuary before September 1: is my plan's current contribution formula capturing everything my age and income allow?

If the answer is no, you have six weeks to fix it.
