### Slide 3 — Step 1: HDHP Check
**Headline:** "Step 1: Is your plan HDHP-qualified?"
**Body:** "Minimum deductible to qualify in 2026: $1,650 (self-only) or $3,300 (family). If your plan's deductible is below these thresholds, you cannot contribute to an HSA — even with an account open."
**Visual:** Deep Muted Blue rounded card; Off-White text; Antique Gold checkmark icon
**Note:** "Price the HDHP option and run the net premium-vs-out-of-pocket analysis before deciding."

### Slide 4 — Step 2: Max the HSA
**Headline:** "Step 2: Are you maxing?"
**Body:** "2026 HSA limits: $4,400 (self-only) / $8,750 (family) / +$1,000 catch-up at 55+. If your plan qualifies and you're not at the maximum, every dollar under the limit is a missed deduction."
**Visual:** Antique Gold accent; Blue Slate card
**Note:** "HSA contributions can be made any time during the year, but eligibility requires HDHP enrollment each month you contribute."

### Slide 5 — Steps 3 & 4: FSA Coordination
**Headline:** "Steps 3 & 4: FSA Election"
**Body:**
- "Standard healthcare FSA: disqualifies HSA eligibility. Decline it if you're maxing the HSA."
- "Limited-purpose FSA (dental/vision only): does NOT disqualify. Can layer on top of the HSA."
- "Dependent care FSA: up to $5,000 per household, pre-tax. Separate from healthcare FSA. Overlooked."
**Visual:** Charcoal rounded card; Off-White text; two-column layout (healthcare FSA left, dependent care FSA right)
