## Content

Does your household have qualifying dependent care expenses?

This question rarely gets asked during open enrollment, which is why the dependent care FSA is the most consistently under-elected benefit in physician benefit packages.

Qualifying expenses include: licensed childcare centers and in-home care for children under 13, before-school and after-school programs, summer day camps, and adult day care for a qualifying dependent. If any of those describe your household's situation, the dependent care FSA offers up to $5,000 per household per year in pre-tax deferrals — a separate vehicle from the healthcare FSA, with its own election.

At the 37% marginal rate, the $5,000 maximum produces $1,850 in federal tax savings per year. It doesn't require an HDHP. It doesn't interact with the HSA. It's a standalone election that most physician households qualify for and most physician households overlook.

The question to ask your benefits coordinator before October: is a dependent care FSA available in my plan, and what are the qualifying expense definitions?
