## Content

Something I noticed walking through a hospital parking structure last week.

Eleven vehicles. Four of them were $80,000 to $150,000. One was a recent $180,000 truck.

A surgeon I was walking with said: "I really need to start saving more."

He's not wrong. But the parking lot is part of the reason he hasn't.

When your professional peer group drives at a certain level, lives in a certain neighborhood, vacations in certain places — your brain registers that as the floor. The minimum acceptable standard. And your spending calibrates to it without you ever consciously deciding.

The parking lot isn't just a place to park. For most high-income surgeons, it's doing more financial planning than their financial plan is.

That's this week's theme. More on Wednesday.
