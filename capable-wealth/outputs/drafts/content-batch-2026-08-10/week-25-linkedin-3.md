## Content

85%. That's the percentage of after-tax income the typical high-income surgeon directs toward lifestyle expenses — not because of reckless decisions, but because the financial structure has no mechanism to capture wealth before it reaches the spending pool.

The structural solution moves the capture point upstream. Three specific interventions:

**One — Cash balance plan.** For a surgeon practice owner in their mid-50s, a properly designed cash balance plan can shelter $150,000 to $300,000 per year in tax-deferred contributions before the W-2 is ever calculated. The money never enters the lifestyle budget. It's already compounding.

**Two — S-Corp salary calibration.** Most surgeons' S-Corp salary was set by a CPA optimizing for payroll tax minimization. That's one variable. The salary also sets the floor for retirement plan contributions. Optimizing for contribution capacity (not just payroll tax) typically unlocks $20,000 to $60,000 in additional annual contribution room.

**Three — Automated distribution transfers.** Practice income hits a business account, then flows to a personal checking account where lifestyle expenses have full access. A standing automated transfer from the distribution point to a taxable investment account — before the money reaches checking — removes the spending decision from the moment entirely.

None of these require discipline at the point of spending. They require one structural decision, made once, that runs automatically.

Which of your three capture points hasn't been reviewed in the last two years?
