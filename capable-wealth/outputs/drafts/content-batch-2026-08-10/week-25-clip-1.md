## Clip Script

[HOOK — first 3 seconds]
$480,000. That's what an $800K surgeon takes home after taxes.

[CORE — approximately 45 seconds]
And the average surgeon is directing about $60,000 of that per year toward wealth accumulation.

The other $420,000? That's working for the parking lot.

Here's the math: at an 87.5% spending rate, $420,000 exits through lifestyle expenses. $60,000 remains to compound.

Now shift to a 75% spending rate. Same income. Same tax rate. The same surgeon.

At 75%, $360,000 covers lifestyle — which, for the record, is an extraordinarily generous life by any objective measure. And $120,000 compounds.

The difference is $60,000 per year.

Over 10 years at 6%, that gap becomes $791,000 in accumulated wealth difference.

[CLOSE — final 5 seconds]
Same income. Same specialty. Two different structures. One of them produces $791,000 more over a decade.

The structure is the decision.
