## Content

There's an old approach to catching raccoons. Put something shiny in a jar with a narrow neck. The raccoon reaches in and grabs it. Its fist won't fit back through the opening. The raccoon could let go and walk away free — but it won't release the thing it's holding.

High-income earners in a lifestyle inflation cycle face a version of this.

The conventional advice is: spend less. Save more. Be more disciplined.

But the money is already in the jar. Lifestyle expenses expand to meet available income because the income reaches the spending pool before any capture mechanism intercepts it.

The structural solution: move the capture point upstream. The money never reaches the jar.

Three specific tools — a cash balance plan, an optimized S-Corp salary structure, and automated distribution transfers — do this automatically. One design decision. No ongoing discipline required.
