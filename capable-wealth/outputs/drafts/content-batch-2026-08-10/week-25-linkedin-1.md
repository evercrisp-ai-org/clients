## Content

$60,000 compounding toward wealth. $420,000 running through the parking lot. That's the average split for an $800K surgeon — and most of them have no idea.

After taxes at roughly 40%, an $800K surgeon works with about $480,000 in after-tax income. At an 87.5% spending rate (which is typical), $420,000 exits as lifestyle expenses. $60,000 remains to compound.

One structural decision changes this.

At a 75% spending rate, the same income, the same taxes, the same surgeon — $360,000 covers lifestyle, and $120,000 compounds. The gap between those two outcomes: $60,000 per year.

Over 10 years at 6%, that gap becomes $791,000 in accumulated difference.

No additional income. No better investments. No change in tax rate.

A financial advisor who hasn't walked through this calculation with you hasn't asked the right question yet. The right question isn't "how are your returns?" It's "what percentage of your after-tax income is building wealth?"

If you don't know that number, you're taking financial advice from your parking lot by default.

What percentage of your after-tax income is actively compounding toward wealth right now?
