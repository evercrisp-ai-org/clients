## Video Script

**[ON CAMERA — Jared, direct to lens, conversational, no notes]**

---

[0:00–0:08 — HOOK]
Seven-thirty Tuesday morning. I'm walking through a hospital parking structure.

I count eleven vehicles. Four of them are $80,000 to $150,000.

And the surgeon walking next to me says: "I've been meaning to save more."

---

[0:08–0:45 — CORE]
Here's what that parking lot is doing that no one talks about.

It's setting the financial benchmark.

Not the market. Not a financial plan. The parking lot.

When your peer group drives a certain vehicle, lives in a certain neighborhood, belongs to a certain club — your brain registers that as the floor. The minimum acceptable standard. And your spending calibrates to it automatically, without a decision ever being made.

The problem isn't the cars. The problem is that the cars are doing financial planning.

For an orthopedic surgeon earning $800,000, this mechanism quietly directs 87% of after-tax income toward lifestyle before a single compounding dollar is captured. Not because of bad values. Because the structure has no override.

---

[0:45–0:62 — CLOSE]
The question worth sitting with this week:

Who is actually setting your financial benchmark right now?

Is it a plan? Or is it the parking lot?
