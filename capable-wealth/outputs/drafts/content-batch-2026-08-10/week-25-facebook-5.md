## Content

Wealth, at the level most surgeons are building toward, isn't primarily about the numbers.

It's about options. The ability to say no to a call schedule that's wearing you down. To take a sabbatical. To transition from full-time clinical work on your own timeline, not the practice's. To make decisions from a position of security rather than one of necessity.

That kind of wealth doesn't happen automatically at $800,000 in income. It happens when the structure is designed to capture it — before lifestyle expenses consume what income doesn't.

The gap between a surgeon who earns $800,000 for 20 years and retires wealthy, and one who earns the same and arrives at 65 with far less than expected, is rarely effort or talent.

It's almost always architecture.

This week was about the architecture. Have a good weekend.
