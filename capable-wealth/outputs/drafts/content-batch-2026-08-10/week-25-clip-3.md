## Clip Script

[HOOK — first 3 seconds]
The raccoon can't grab what it can't reach.

[CORE — approximately 50 seconds]
If your spending problem is structural — and for most high-income surgeons, it is — the fix is structural. Not willpower. Architecture.

What does that mean in practice? Three specific things.

One: a cash balance plan that captures $150,000 to $300,000 per year in tax-deferred contributions before your W-2 is ever calculated. That money doesn't exist in your lifestyle budget. It's already gone — into a compounding vehicle.

Two: S-Corp salary calibration designed around contribution capacity, not just payroll tax minimization. The right salary setting typically unlocks $20,000 to $60,000 more in annual contribution room. Most surgeons are optimizing for the wrong variable.

Three: an automated transfer from practice distributions to a taxable investment account before those distributions hit your primary checking account. The money moves before you see it.

None of these require discipline in the moment. The decision is made once, structurally, and then executed automatically.

[CLOSE — final 5 seconds]
That's the difference between fighting the spending trap every day and designing your way out of it.
