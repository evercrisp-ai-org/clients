## Content

Three structural interventions. One decision each. Zero ongoing discipline required.

**Cash balance plan:** For a surgeon practice owner in their mid-50s, this can shelter $150,000 to $300,000 per year in tax-deferred contributions before the W-2 is even calculated. The money never enters the lifestyle budget.

**S-Corp salary optimization:** Most surgeon S-Corp salaries were set to minimize payroll taxes. Smart. But the same salary determines how much you can contribute to retirement plans. Optimizing for contribution room (not just payroll tax) typically unlocks $20,000 to $60,000 more per year.

**Automated distribution transfer:** Set a standing transfer from practice distributions to a taxable investment account — before the money reaches your personal checking account. The spending decision is removed from the moment entirely.

Together, these three moves can capture $200,000 or more per year before lifestyle expenses ever reach it. No behavior change required at the spending level.

That's what architecture does that discipline can't.
