## Clip Script

[HOOK — first 3 seconds]
Your 401(k) contribution of $32,500 per year is less than 7% of your gross income.

[CORE — approximately 40 seconds]
That doesn't mean you're doing anything wrong. Most surgeons contribute to a 401(k) and feel like they're saving responsibly. They are.

But here's what most of them don't know: there's a second vehicle that could shelter $150,000 to $300,000 per year in tax-deferred contributions — on top of the 401(k).

A cash balance plan. Defined benefit structure layered with your 401(k). For a surgeon in their mid-50s, properly designed, the combined contribution capacity can exceed $350,000 per year.

Most surgeon practice owners have never set one up.

The 401(k) feels like savings. The cash balance plan gap is a silent drain — not because money is leaving your account, but because it could have been sheltered and it wasn't.

[CLOSE — final 5 seconds]
$32,500 and $300,000 both qualify as "saving." Only one is at scale.
