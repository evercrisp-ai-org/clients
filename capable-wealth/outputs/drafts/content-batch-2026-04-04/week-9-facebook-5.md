This week has been full of practice transition conversations. Conference hallways, LinkedIn posts, dinner tables. A lot of information coming at you from a lot of directions.

Here's the simplest takeaway I can offer: if you're within five years of potentially thinking about what's next (and "potentially" is the key word), schedule one conversation with your financial advisor this month.

Not to make a decision. Not to commit to anything. Just to establish a baseline.

A 30-minute conversation that covers three things: What is your practice actually worth today? What does your current entity structure look like? And what would need to happen over the next few years to put you in a position of strength if you ever do want to explore a transition?

That single conversation, with no strings attached, is the Year 5 starting point. Everything else builds from it. And it costs you nothing but 30 minutes.

The surgeons who feel most at peace about their financial futures aren't the ones who have everything figured out. They're the ones who started asking the right questions early enough to not need the answers right away.

One conversation. This month. That's the whole move.
