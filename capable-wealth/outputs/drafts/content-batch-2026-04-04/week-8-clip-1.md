## Content

### Caption Script

Your CPA is great at compliance. Your advisor is great at investments. Your attorney is great at estate documents. But who's looking at the whole picture?

In most cases, nobody.

Your S-Corp salary affects your retirement plan capacity. Your retirement plan capacity affects your tax bill. Your tax strategy affects which accounts your investments should sit in. These are interconnected systems.

And here's what's expensive about the gap: each professional optimizes their piece. The CPA keeps the salary low to save on payroll taxes. But that lower salary caps the cash balance plan contribution. The payroll tax savings might be $9,000 a year. The lost retirement plan capacity might be $40,000.

The problem isn't that your advisors are bad. The problem is that they're working in silos.

A financial second opinion reads the full composite study, not the individual images. It looks at how entity, retirement, tax, and investment interact as a single system.

That's where the value lives.
