## Content

### Caption Script

The difference between a $4.2 million exit and a $2.8 million exit is three years of planning.

Here's how. Consider a surgeon at 55 with a practice valued at $2.8 million. With a 5-year timeline, three things happen that don't happen in a 2-year sprint.

First, entity restructuring and personal goodwill documentation. That alone can save $300,000 to $500,000 in taxes on the sale, because personal goodwill is taxed at capital gains rates instead of ordinary income.

Second, revenue diversification and associate recruitment. Adding an ASC stake and proving the practice runs without the founder pushes the valuation multiple from 3x to 4x EBITDA.

Third, competitive buyer positioning. When you have five years, you attract multiple offers. When you have two years, you take what's available.

The result: $4.2 million at 60 with the 5-year plan, versus $2.8 million in a compressed sale. A $1.4 million difference. Same surgeon, same practice. The only variable is when the planning started.
