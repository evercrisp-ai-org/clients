Something worth sitting with as the week wraps up, especially if you've been hearing a lot about practice transitions at conferences this spring.

The surgeons who feel most in control of their professional and financial lives aren't the ones who got the best deal. They're the ones who had time to choose.

Time to evaluate whether a PE offer was right for them, or just flattering. Time to document the personal goodwill that saves hundreds of thousands in taxes. Time to diversify their revenue so the practice's value doesn't depend entirely on their hands. Time to build a transition plan that reflects what they actually want, not just what's available.

That time isn't something you find. You create it. By starting the planning years before you think you need it.

Your practice is likely the biggest financial bet you've ever made. You built it with precision. You grew it with skill. The way you transition out of it (or restructure your relationship with it) deserves the same intentionality.

Whether you're five years from a sale or fifteen, the question is the same: do you have options, or are you waiting for one to appear?

The best time to create options is long before you need them. That's not urgency. That's just good planning.
