## Content

### Caption Script

You've ordered hundreds of second opinions in your career. Complex fracture patterns. Ambiguous MRI findings. A surgical plan that doesn't quite sit right.

You pick up the phone. You send the images. You ask a colleague to take a fresh look.

You don't do this because you doubt yourself. You do it because the stakes are high enough that a second perspective makes the outcome better. The value of a second opinion isn't in contradicting the first reading. It's in seeing what the first reader might have missed.

Now, here's the question. Have you ever applied that same discipline to your financial plan?

At surgeon income levels, the difference between an optimized and an unoptimized financial structure can be $50,000 to $80,000 per year. Over a decade, that compounds into a fundamentally different retirement.

You already know why second opinions work. You've been on both sides of them. The same rigor that makes you a better surgeon can make your financial plan better, too.
