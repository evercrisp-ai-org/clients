Now that April 15 has passed, here's one question worth asking your financial advisor before the tax chapter closes completely:

"When was the last time someone looked at how my CPA's tax strategy, my investment advisor's portfolio approach, and my estate plan work together as a single system?"

Most of the time, the honest answer is: never.

That's not a failure on anyone's part. CPAs focus on tax compliance. Advisors focus on investments. Attorneys focus on estate documents. Each one does their job well. But the coordination between those domains is where the biggest planning opportunities tend to live for surgeons earning $700,000 or more.

The post-tax-season window (now through October) is the natural time for this kind of review. No deadline pressure. Current-year data coming in. Enough runway to implement changes that matter for this year.

One question. One call. It could be the most productive financial conversation you have this spring.
