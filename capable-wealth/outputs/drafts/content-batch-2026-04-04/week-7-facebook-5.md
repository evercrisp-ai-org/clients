Seven weeks of content. One message.

We started with the structural gaps that cost surgeons the most: entity misalignment, S-Corp salary calibration, the overhead question nobody asks. We moved to what the CMS reimbursement cut actually means for your take-home, and the one number (your effective hourly rate) that tells you whether your financial structure is working.

Then we shifted to what your tax return reveals when you read it like a diagnostic report, the three decisions hiding inside April 15, and now, the six-month planning window that opens the day after the deadline.

The thread running through all of it? The amount your practice earns and the amount you keep are two very different numbers. Understanding the gap between them, and building a structure to close it, is where real financial planning begins.

Next month, the conversation shifts. From how to keep more of what you earn to what happens when the practice itself becomes the asset. Practice transition, exit planning, valuation, and the question most surgeons don't ask early enough.

If anything from the last seven weeks stuck with you, the simplest next step is the same one it's been since Week 1: one coordinated conversation with someone who can look at the full picture.
