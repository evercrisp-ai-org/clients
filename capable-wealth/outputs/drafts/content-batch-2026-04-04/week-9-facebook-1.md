Conference season is kicking off, and if you're in orthopedics, practice transition will be the conversation in every hallway and at every dinner.

My advice: listen, learn, and take notes. But don't make any decisions at the conference.

The best practice exits are planned over years, not decided over dinner. The PE pitch deck might be polished. The multiple might sound compelling. The colleague who just sold might make it sound easy. All of that is worth hearing. None of it should be acted on in the moment.

Give yourself a 5-year runway, and you'll have choices: entity restructuring, personal goodwill documentation, revenue diversification, competitive buyer positioning. All of the moves that create the most value need time.

Rush it, and you settle for whatever's available.

The surgeons who walk away from conferences with the best outcomes aren't the ones who signed something. They're the ones who came home, scheduled a planning conversation, and started building a protocol on their own timeline.

Enjoy the conversations this season. Just make sure the decisions happen later, when you've had time to think.
