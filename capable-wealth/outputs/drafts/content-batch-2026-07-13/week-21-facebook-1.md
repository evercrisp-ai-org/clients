## Content

Has anyone told you that your annual RVU output now compares directly to the hospital benchmark when a buyer values your practice?

Most independent orthopedic surgeons know that hospital systems have been investing in scheduling infrastructure. What's often missing is that this investment has moved the benchmark buyers use to stress-test practice valuations.

Hospital-employed orthopedists now generate more work RVUs per year than independent practice owners, a reversal from where the benchmark sat for most of the past decade.

For practice owners in the 3-to-10-year window before a potential transition, this has a direct financial implication. A practice that tracks at or above the hospital benchmark holds its valuation. One that tracks below it gets a revenue adjustment in the buyer's model before terms are set.

Your own number is worth knowing. When did you last compare your RVU output to the MGMA benchmark for your subspecialty?
