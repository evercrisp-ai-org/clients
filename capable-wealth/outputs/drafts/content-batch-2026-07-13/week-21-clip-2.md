## Clip Script

[HOOK — first 3 seconds]
The hospital RVU shift just moved $560,000 out of one surgeon's exit outcome. Before a single negotiation started.

[CORE CONTENT — 40-50 seconds]
Here's the math. Consider a practice with $1.45 million in normalized EBITDA. At a 4x multiple, that practice is worth $5.8 million.

Now a buyer runs their productivity stress test. They model the forward revenue assuming a replacement surgeon who operates at the current hospital benchmark, rather than at the selling surgeon's personal output level. That 10 percent revenue adjustment reduces the normalized EBITDA to $1.31 million. At 4x, the practice is now worth $5.24 million.

$560,000 in practice value. Gone. The cause is a market benchmark that shifted and a practice that hadn't adapted to it.

The surgeons who are best positioned for exit right now are the ones whose productivity data tells the story of an independence premium. They outperform the hospital benchmark, which signals to every buyer that their clinical volume and referral relationships are personal and transferable.

[CLOSING BEAT — 5-8 seconds]
Your productivity data is telling a story right now. The question is whether you know what it says.
