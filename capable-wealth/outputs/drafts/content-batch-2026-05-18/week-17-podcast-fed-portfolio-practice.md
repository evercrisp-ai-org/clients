## COLD OPEN (30-60 seconds)

$30K a year moves with every 1% on a $3M portfolio. That is the kind of number that gets attention because it turns a planning concept into something you can feel.

Today we are going to take one issue that looks technical and turn it into a practical diagnostic for a surgeon practice owner. The goal is to know which decision deserves attention this week, even while the future remains uncertain.

---
## INTRO AND CONTEXT (1-2 minutes)

Uses the June 16-17 FOMC meeting as a timely hook while avoiding claims about a decision that has not happened yet.

The reason this matters for surgeons is simple: the numbers are large, the calendar is unforgiving, and the planning windows close before most people feel ready. At this income level, delay is rarely neutral.

---
## SEGMENT 1: Waiting Is a Decision (3-5 minutes)

`[CLIP-CANDIDATE]`

The Federal Reserve meeting this week will generate headlines, predictions, and confident commentary. A surgeon can make good decisions without predicting the next rate move. Waiting for certainty is still a decision, and it often leaves cash, debt, and portfolios drifting.

The practical question is what this means for the decisions in front of you right now. A good plan makes the next step obvious before the deadline makes it urgent.

---
## SEGMENT 2: Where Rates Actually Touch Your Life (3-5 minutes)

`[CLIP-CANDIDATE]`

Rates show up in four practical places: cash yield, bond portfolio positioning, mortgage and practice debt, and valuation assumptions for practice transitions. Each deserves a review. Each deserves a deliberate response.

On a $3,000,000 portfolio, a 1% allocation or yield difference is $30,000 per year before tax. On a $750,000 practice loan, a 1% financing difference is $7,500 per year. Rate decisions matter, but they should feed a framework rather than trigger reactive moves.

---
## SEGMENT 3: Build the If-Then Framework (3-5 minutes)

`[CLIP-CANDIDATE]`

If rates stay higher, cash and short-duration fixed income may remain useful, but debt costs need attention. If rates fall, refinancing windows and reinvestment risk come back into view. If markets react sharply, tax-loss harvesting or rebalancing may become available. The point is to pre-decide the framework before the headlines arrive.

The practical question is what this means for the decisions in front of you right now. A good plan makes the next step obvious before the deadline makes it urgent.

---
## SEGMENT 4: What To Review This Week (3-5 minutes)

Review your cash yield, fixed-income allocation, variable-rate debt, upcoming borrowing needs, and any concentrated positions with embedded gains or losses. The Fed meeting is the calendar prompt. The planning work is yours.

The practical question is what this means for the decisions in front of you right now. A good plan makes the next step obvious before the deadline makes it urgent.

---
## LISTENER TAKEAWAY (1-2 minutes)

One action this week: pull the numbers connected to this topic and place them on one page. If the page reveals a gap, name the decision, assign an owner, and set the next review date. That is how a planning rhythm begins.

---
## PHILOSOPHICAL CLOSE (1-2 minutes)

Financial planning works best when structure lets you make deliberate decisions while everyone else is guessing at headlines or deadlines.

That structure is what turns high income into control. And control is the point.
