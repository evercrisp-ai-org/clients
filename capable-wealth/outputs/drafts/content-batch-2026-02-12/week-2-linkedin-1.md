Your CPA told you to keep your S-Corp salary low. That advice might be costing you $25K a year.

Here's why.

The "low salary" strategy saves payroll taxes. That's true. At surgeon-level income, the Medicare savings between a $300K salary and a $360K salary is about $5,200 per year.

But here's what nobody shows you:

Your 401(k) employer match and cash balance plan contributions are based on your W-2 compensation. Set the salary too low, and you cap your retirement plan capacity.

At $300K salary → ~$115K in total retirement contributions.
At $360K salary → ~$155K in total retirement contributions.

That's a $40K gap. In tax-deferred wealth. Every year.

You're saving $5,200 in payroll taxes to lose $40,000 in tax-sheltered compounding.

That math doesn't work at any income level. At $745K, it's painful.

The right S-Corp salary isn't the lowest one. It's the one that puts the most money to work across all three variables: payroll taxes, retirement contributions, and the QBI deduction.

If your salary hasn't been recalculated in the last two years, this is the quarter to revisit it.
