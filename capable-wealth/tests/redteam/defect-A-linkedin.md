## Content

There is a question worth asking every surgeon who owns a practice. Most of you set up an S-Corp years ago and never revisited it, which is completely understandable given how busy a surgical practice keeps you and how rarely anyone tells you to look again.

Here is the thing nobody mentions. Your entity structure is not a one-time decision, it is a living part of your financial plan that should change as your income grows. The structure that made sense at $400K in collections is often the wrong structure at $900K, and the gap compounds quietly year after year while you focus on patients.

This is not about working harder, but about keeping more of what you already earn. A reasonable-compensation analysis, a look at whether a cash balance plan belongs on top of your 401(k), a review of how your distributions are characterized, these are the moves that separate surgeons who keep their income from surgeons who hand an extra slice to the IRS every April without ever noticing.

I have walked through this with a number of practice owners and the reaction is almost always the same, a mix of surprise and mild frustration that nobody raised it sooner. The good news is that it is fixable, and the fix usually pays for itself many times over in the first year alone.
