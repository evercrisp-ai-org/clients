# Experience Interview Guide

> This document captures the real context behind Jared and Capable Wealth. Its purpose is to give the content system an accurate foundation so that every piece of content is true, compliant, and resonant. Answer each question honestly. Short answers are fine. "Not yet" and "N/A" are perfectly valid. The content system will use these answers to determine what can be stated as fact, what should be framed as illustrative, and what must be avoided entirely.

**How to use this guide:**
- Work through each section at your own pace. Some sections may take reflection.
- After completing it, the responses will be synthesized into [experience-inventory.md](experience-inventory.md), which the AI references during content creation.
- Review and update this document at least quarterly, or whenever something material changes (new client engagement, new credential, new service offering).

**Companion documents:**
- [experience-inventory.md](experience-inventory.md): The structured reference document populated from these responses
- [voice-profile.md](voice-profile.md): WHO Jared is as a communicator
- [content-recipe.md](content-recipe.md): HOW content gets produced, including the Content Integrity Filter

## Section 1: Professional Timeline

These questions establish the verifiable facts about your career. Content can reference these directly.

1. When did you earn your CFP designation? (Month/year)

2. How many total years have you worked in financial services?

3. What firms or roles did you hold before launching Capable Wealth? (Firm names optional; role descriptions and durations are what matter for content credibility.)

4. When did Capable Wealth officially launch? (Month/year)

5. What licenses, registrations, or designations do you currently hold beyond CFP? (Series 65, RIA registration, insurance licenses, etc.)

6. Are there any professional milestones or recognitions worth noting? (Awards, publications, speaking engagements, board positions, etc.)

---

## Section 2: Current Client Base

These questions define what the content system can honestly say about your advisory practice today. No names needed.

7. Approximately how many active clients do you currently serve?

8. What industries or professions do your current clients represent? (e.g., small business owners, executives, medical professionals, tech, etc.)

9. What is the general income range of your current client base? (e.g., $150K-$500K, $500K-$1M, varies widely)

10. What is the general net worth range of your current client base?

11. How long have you been working with your longest-tenured client?

12. What services do you provide most frequently today? (Financial planning, tax strategy, retirement planning, investment management, estate planning, etc.)

13. Do you work with any clients in a team or co-advisory capacity with other professionals (CPAs, attorneys, etc.)?

## Section 3: Orthopedic Surgeon and Physician Experience

These questions determine how the content system handles surgeon-specific claims. This is the area where the most fabrication risk exists.

14. Have you worked with any orthopedic surgeons as clients? If yes, how many and for how long?

15. Have you worked with any physicians or medical professionals (any specialty) as clients? If yes, how many, what specialties, and for how long?

16. Have you worked with any high-income practice owners (medical or otherwise) whose financial structures are similar to surgeon-owned practices? If yes, describe generally.

17. What is your plan for entering the orthopedic surgeon niche? Are you actively prospecting, networking, or building referral relationships in this space?

18. Have you attended any orthopedic or medical industry conferences, events, or networking groups? (e.g., AAOS, state medical society events)

19. Do you have any personal connections to the orthopedic or medical community? (Friends, family, referral partners, etc.)

## Section 4: Real Outcomes and Case Studies

These become the "verified stories" vault. Each story you provide here can be used in content (anonymized and with details changed for privacy). Be as specific as you're comfortable with. If you don't have stories in a category, say so.

**For each story, provide:**
- Client type (industry, approximate income level, life stage)
- The problem or structural gap you identified
- What you recommended
- The measurable outcome (dollar savings, tax reduction, contribution increase, etc.)
- Approximate timeframe (when this happened, how long the engagement lasted)

20. Describe any real client scenarios where you identified a significant tax structure optimization. (S-Corp salary calibration, entity restructuring, missed deductions, etc.)

21. Describe any real client scenarios involving retirement plan optimization. (Cash balance plans, 401(k) maximization, defined benefit plans, catch-up contributions, etc.)

22. Describe any real client scenarios involving practice or business transition/succession planning.

23. Describe any real client scenarios involving estate planning or wealth transfer strategies.

24. Describe any other real client outcomes that you're proud of and that demonstrate your value as an advisor.

25. Are there any client outcomes or stories that are still in progress (not yet fully realized) but that you expect to produce measurable results?

## Section 5: Knowledge vs. Experience

**For each topic below, indicate your level:**
- **Practiced**: You have directly implemented this with one or more clients and can point to a real outcome.
- **Studied**: You understand this deeply through CFP curriculum, continuing education, self-study, or research, but have not yet executed it with a client.
- **Familiar**: You have a working understanding but would need to deepen your knowledge before advising on it.

## Section 6: Personal Anecdotes

The voice profile already captures several personal stories (welfare upbringing, caddying, $1 microwaveable dinners, the day you stopped living paycheck-to-paycheck, your brother's spending habits). These are powerful because they're real.

29. Are there additional personal financial moments or milestones you'd be comfortable sharing in content? (e.g., first major purchase, a financial mistake, a turning point in how you thought about money)

## Section 7: Aspirational Positioning

These questions define where Capable Wealth is heading. This determines what content can frame as "building toward" vs. what it can frame as "established."

33. What is your ideal client profile in 12 months? In 3 years?

34. What specific services do you plan to offer to orthopedic surgeons that you don't currently provide?

35. Are you building any referral or strategic partnerships in the medical/orthopedic space? (CPAs who serve surgeons, medical practice consultants, etc.)

36. Are there thought leadership goals you're working toward? (Speaking at AAOS, publishing in medical practice journals, hosting surgeon-specific events, etc.)

37. Is there a specific number of surgeon clients you're targeting within a defined timeframe?

38. How would you describe Capable Wealth's current stage? (e.g., "established practice entering a new niche," "new practice building from scratch," "existing practice pivoting focus")

---

## Section 8: Disclosure Boundaries

These questions define what must never be implied, approximated, or referenced in content.

39. Are there any prior employers, clients, or professional relationships that should never be referenced or hinted at?

40. Are there any financial topics you do not want to be associated with in content, even in an educational context?

41. Are there any personal details from your life that are off-limits for content, even if they're referenced in the existing blog posts?

42. Are there any compliance or regulatory constraints specific to your registration (RIA, broker-dealer, state regulations) that impose limits on what content can claim? (e.g., restrictions on testimonials, performance advertising, specific disclaimers required)

43. Is there anything else the content system should know about what to avoid?
