**Relevance: Bridge from Week 21 sharpens the urgency here significantly.**

The Week 23 succession planning piece should open from that finding rather than ignoring it. The argument is tighter: if the practice productivity benchmark is moving against you, the 3-year runway for succession planning just got shorter in effective terms. A practice selling in three years that hasn't addressed the RVU gap will face buyer scrutiny on the forward revenue model.

**Angle refinements:**

## Week 24: August 3–9

**Editorial plan source:** Month 6 (August 2026), Content 3
**Primary channel:** LinkedIn (full week; anchor supplementary)

### Freshness Assessment

**Timing: Well-positioned. Open enrollment prep 2 months ahead of the enrollment window.**

Most practices hold open enrollment in October–November. Publishing the open enrollment tax strategy piece in early August gives practice-owner surgeons 6–8 weeks to evaluate their benefits structure before the enrollment window opens. The lead time is exactly right: decisions like adding a group disability policy or adjusting the executive benefit structure require underwriting time.

**Relevance: Stronger framing available — link open enrollment to the year-end tax strategy arc.**

The editorial plan frames this as a LinkedIn-primary piece about maximizing the tax value of benefits elections. The framing should be sharpened for the specific position in the arc: weeks 22–24 are all about August moves with year-end payoff. Open enrollment fits this arc precisely — benefits elections made in October lock in tax advantages (or miss them) for the full calendar year.

Proposed retitle from "Open Enrollment Is a Tax Strategy — Not Just an HR Headache" to: **"Your Open Enrollment Decision Will Cost or Save You $30K–$50K This Year. Most Surgeon-Owners Don't Know That."**

This is sharper loss framing and gives the LinkedIn hook an immediate hook quantity that performs well.

**Angle refinements:**
- The core argument from the editorial plan is unchanged and strong: benefits elections are a tax deduction menu for practice-owner surgeons, not a checkbox exercise.
- Key elements to emphasize:
  1. **HSA maximization:** $8,750 family + $1,000 catch-up at 55+. At a 37% marginal rate, the after-tax value of maxing the HSA is $3,638 in immediate tax savings plus tax-free compounding. Surgeons who don't max this leave money on the table in the most straightforward way possible.
  2. **Group life insurance structuring (Section 79):** Up to $50K of employer-paid group term life insurance is excludable from income. Practice-owner surgeons can often structure coverage above this threshold through split-dollar or executive bonus arrangements that shift the tax treatment. This requires lead time to design and underwrite.
  3. **Executive disability:** Own-occupation disability coverage for the surgeon as a practice owner should be reviewed during open enrollment. Premiums paid by the practice may be deductible; the design of the policy affects whether benefits are taxable if received.
  4. **Retirement plan contribution coordination:** Benefits elections determine W-2 compensation, which affects contribution limits for the cash balance plan. If the surgeon is planning a salary adjustment (from the Week 22 discussion), open enrollment is the coordination moment.
- Connect to succession planning (Week 23): for a surgeon 3 years from exit, the benefits structure established during this open enrollment cycle will appear in the practice's financials that a buyer eventually normalizes. Structuring benefits as legitimate, deductible business expenses now strengthens the normalized EBITDA picture.

**Continuity:** The three-week arc flows cleanly: Week 22 (start year-end tax moves now) → Week 23 (start succession planning now) → Week 24 (optimize your open enrollment, which touches both). This is a tightly integrated set of August "start now" moves.

**No significant FOMC dependency this week.** The Fed decision lands in Week 23. By Week 24, any rate decision is established context rather than breaking news.

### Verify Before Publish

- **2026 HSA limits:** Confirmed in editorial plan at $8,750 family / $4,400 individual / $1,000 catch-up at 55+. Verify IRS has not issued a mid-year correction (rare but possible).
- **Section 79 group term life exclusion:** Confirm the $50K employer-paid group term life exclusion limit is unchanged for 2026.
- **ACA employer mandate thresholds:** If the piece references practice size in the context of required vs. optional benefits, confirm the current ALE threshold (typically 50 full-time equivalent employees). Most surgeon practices are below this; worth noting if referenced.
- **Executive disability deductibility:** Verify that premiums paid by the practice for individual disability coverage remain deductible as a business expense in 2026 (no OBBBA changes expected, but confirm).
