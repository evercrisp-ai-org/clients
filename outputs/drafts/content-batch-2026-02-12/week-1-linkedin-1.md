Here's a frustration that comes up constantly for surgeons: the tax bill feels too high. And almost every time, the real issue lands in the same place. Not the tax code. The entity structure.

Consider a surgeon making $800K, convinced he's "getting killed on taxes."

The CPA is doing everything right. The issue is further upstream, in how the income flows through the entity.

Here's the difference:

Surgeon A earns $800K. Pays taxes. Takes home roughly $480K.

Surgeon B earns the same $800K. Same tax code. Same state. Takes home $560K.

The gap? $80K a year.

Not because of some clever deduction. Because of three structural decisions:

→ How the S-Corp salary is calibrated
→ Whether a cash balance plan exists
→ Whether real estate depreciation has been accelerated

These aren't exotic strategies. They're foundational. And they're the difference between a high income and real wealth.

The tax code doesn't change based on who your accountant is. But how much you keep absolutely changes based on how your income flows through your entity structure.

If you're an orthopedic practice owner earning $700K+ and you've never had your entity structure stress-tested (not your tax return reviewed, but your actual structure evaluated) that's the highest-leverage conversation you can have this quarter.

Once the structure is right, the tax bill takes care of itself.
