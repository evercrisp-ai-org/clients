Most surgeon practice owners think of their S-Corp salary as a payroll decision. Set it, run payroll, move on.

But that one number quietly drives three different outcomes in your financial life:

1. Your payroll taxes (the one everyone knows about).
2. Your retirement plan contributions. Your 401(k) and cash balance plan are both tied to your W-2 compensation. Lower salary, lower contributions.
3. Your QBI deduction. The 20% pass-through deduction depends on the ratio of your salary to total practice income.

Optimize for one and you often make the other two worse.

At $745K in practice income, the difference between a well-calibrated salary and one that was set three years ago can be $15K to $25K per year in wealth-building capacity.

One number. Three consequences. Worth a closer look this quarter.
