I had a conversation this week that I keep coming back to.

An orthopedic surgeon (great practice, strong income) told me he was frustrated that he wasn't further along financially. His first instinct: "I need a better accountant."

I hear this all the time. And the answer is almost never a better accountant.

It's a better structure.

How your practice income flows through your entity (your S-Corp setup, your retirement plans, your depreciation strategy) is what determines how much you actually keep.

One question worth asking your advisor this week: "When was the last time we stress-tested my entity structure?"

Not reviewed my return. Stress-tested my structure.

There's a difference. And at surgeon-level income, that difference can be six figures a year.
