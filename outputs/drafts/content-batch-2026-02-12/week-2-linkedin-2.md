There are three numbers fighting for control of your S-Corp salary. Most surgeons only know about one of them.

The one everyone knows: payroll taxes. Lower salary = lower taxes. Simple.

The one most people miss: retirement plan contributions. Your 401(k) match and cash balance plan are both based on your W-2. Lower salary = lower contributions. Not simple.

The one almost nobody considers: the QBI deduction. Your 20% pass-through deduction depends on the ratio of salary to total income. Too high or too low, and you leave money on the table.

These three variables interact.

Optimizing for just one (which is what most CPAs do, because their job is compliance, not optimization) means the other two are suboptimal by default.

The result? A surgeon earning $745K who's been running on the same "reasonable compensation" for years is almost certainly in the wrong spot.

Not because the CPA made an error. Because nobody modeled all three variables together.

One annual conversation can fix this. But it has to be the right conversation: not "is my return correct?" but "is my structure optimal?"
