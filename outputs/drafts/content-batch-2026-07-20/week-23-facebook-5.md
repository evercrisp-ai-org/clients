## Content

One question worth sitting with this weekend:

If you had to sell your practice in 18 months — not because you chose to, but because the market moved and the decision was made for you — would it be ready?

Not "would you accept the number." Ready. In the sense that the personal goodwill is documented, the referral network is mapped, an associate is generating independent revenue, and the practice's EBITDA isn't 90% dependent on your personal surgical relationships.

For most orthopedic practice owners between 50 and 57, the honest answer is no. And the three-year succession runway — if it starts this August — is how that answer changes by 2029.

The goal isn't to sell faster. It's to make sure that when the time comes, the practice is ready on your terms, not the buyer's.
