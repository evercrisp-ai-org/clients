## Content

"When the time comes" is the most expensive succession planning strategy in medicine.

The time always comes faster than expected, and with less warning than the surgeon assumed. And the practices that sell on the owner's terms — with the highest multiples, the cleanest tax treatment, and the most favorable earnout structures — are the ones where the time came long before the sale.

The three-year succession runway is not about being eager to leave. Most surgeons who start it in their mid-50s aren't. It's about recognizing that the work that protects practice value — reducing surgeon-dependence, documenting personal goodwill, building an associate revenue track record — requires years of lead time to show up in the financials a buyer reviews.

August is succession planning season. The surgeons who will exit well in 2028 and 2029 are starting the first year of that protocol right now.
