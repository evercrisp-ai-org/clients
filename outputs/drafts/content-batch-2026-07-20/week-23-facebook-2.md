## Content

If you run an independent orthopedic practice and you're within five years of a potential exit, one data shift from this year deserves your attention.

Hospital and health system-employed orthopedic surgeons now outperform independent practices on median work RVU output. This is a recent reversal of a long-standing pattern.

It matters at the closing table because of how buyers model post-close revenue. When a buyer evaluates a practice, they run a replacement surgeon scenario: what does this practice generate without the selling surgeon? That model now uses the hospital productivity benchmark as a reference point. If a buyer's analyst concludes that a replacement surgeon will generate 10 to 15% fewer RVUs than the hospital benchmark, the practice's forward revenue projection takes a discount before the first term sheet is written.

The practical question this raises: how much of your practice's revenue is genuinely transferable, and how much leaves when you do?

That ratio — surgeon-dependent revenue versus transferable revenue — is what the three-year succession runway is designed to shift before the sale.
