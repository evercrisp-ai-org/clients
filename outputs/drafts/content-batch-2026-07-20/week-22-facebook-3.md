## Content

Picture this: a surgeon establishes a cash balance plan at 52. Income at the time: $720,000. The plan is designed for $110,000 in annual contributions — appropriate, well-structured, right for where she was.

At 57, her income is $950,000. Her plan is still set to $110,000.

The plan has room to expand — based on her age and current compensation, potentially to $165,000 per year. But expanding requires a plan amendment. And the amendment has to be filed before October 1 for most calendar-year plans.

The process takes six to eight weeks once initiated.

If she starts the conversation with her actuary in July or August: the amendment is adopted before the deadline. An additional $55,000 per year flows into tax-deferred compounding. At 37%, that is $20,350 in immediate federal tax savings annually.

If she starts in October: the window is closed for 2026.

One question to ask your actuary before August 31: has my plan's contribution formula kept pace with my income growth?

If the answer is no, August is the time to fix it.
