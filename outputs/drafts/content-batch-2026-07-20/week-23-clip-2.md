## Script

**[HOOK — on camera]**

The documentation has to be established before negotiations begin.

Once you're in a deal process and a buyer is at the table, the personal goodwill allocation argument is nearly impossible to win. Because the buyer has every incentive to push back on it.

**[Brief pause — let the point land]**

Here's what personal goodwill actually is. It's the portion of your practice's value attributable to your individual reputation, your clinical relationships, and your referral network — the things that leave the practice when you do.

When properly documented, that portion is taxed as long-term capital gains: 20% plus 3.8% NIIT. Enterprise goodwill — the part that stays with the entity — is typically taxed at higher blended rates.

**[Back to direct address]**

The documentation process involves a valuation professional. You're separating and quantifying the personal component: referral source attribution, patient loyalty analysis, non-compete scope, your own assessment of what the practice loses when you're gone.

The surgeon who documented three years before the sale has a paper trail. The surgeon who tries to establish it during the deal is negotiating against herself.

If you're three years out — the documentation process starts now.
