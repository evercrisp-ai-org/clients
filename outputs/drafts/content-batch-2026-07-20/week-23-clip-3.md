## Script

**[HOOK — on camera]**

$3.2 million versus $1.7 million. Same practice. Same EBITDA.

The difference is three years of runway work.

**[Lean slightly forward — the math is the content]**

Here's how that math works. Consider a surgeon at 55 planning to exit at 58. Current EBITDA: $900,000.

With a three-year runway: the practice becomes 65% transferable — an associate adds $300,000 in non-surgeon-dependent EBITDA — personal goodwill is documented at 45% of the transaction. Exit at $1.1 million EBITDA, 4x multiple: $4.4 million enterprise value. After tax on properly allocated goodwill: $3.2 million net.

Without the runway — an 18-month distressed sale, no systematization, no documentation: $900,000 EBITDA, 3x multiple, taxed almost entirely as ordinary income. Net: $1.7 million.

**[Pull back — broader frame]**

That $1.5 million gap isn't strategy. It isn't investment returns. It's timing — specifically, when the runway started.

If you're between 50 and 57 and this conversation hasn't begun, the math above is roughly what's at stake.

The runway starts in August.
