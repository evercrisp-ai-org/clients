## Content

The benefits packet is not a benefits administration document. It's the annual tax planning window that disguises itself as paperwork.

Most physicians treat open enrollment as something to get through — confirm the dependents, pick the same plan as last year, submit before the deadline. The result is that the HSA goes unfunded because the HDHP analysis never happens. The dependent care FSA never gets elected because the question was never asked.

For an orthopedic surgeon at the 37% marginal rate, the combination of a maxed HSA ($9,750 at 55+) and a dependent care FSA ($5,000) produces $5,458 in annual federal tax savings from two enrollment decisions. That's before the compounded growth in the HSA account.

The enrollment window opens in October. The decision should be made in August.

Treat the benefits packet like a tax document. Review it with your advisor before the deadline arrives. The annual cost of not doing so — in missed deductions and foregone compounding — is typically larger than anyone realizes until they look.
