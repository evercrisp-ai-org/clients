### Caption Script

Add up the full stack and a surgeon in their fifties can defer past $300,000 a year.

Start with the 401(k) elective deferral, $24,500, plus an $8,000 catch-up at 50 and older.

Layer the employer and profit-sharing pieces, and the defined contribution side can reach $72,000.

Then add the cash balance plan on top, designed for $150,000 to $350,000 depending on age.

Stacked together, that is the most powerful wealth-building structure a practice owner has. It only works when each layer is funded on schedule, which is exactly why a midyear check earns its keep.
