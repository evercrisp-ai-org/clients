3 numbers confirm whether your cash balance plan is actually on pace.

This is a fifteen-minute check, and it is worth doing this month rather than at year-end. Pull three figures and put them side by side.

One, the plan's annual target. The contribution your plan is designed and committed to fund this year.

Two, where you should be on the schedule right now. Roughly half the annual target by mid-July for a level-funded plan.

Three, what has actually gone in. The real dollars contributed through payroll to date, not the amount you intended.

If number three matches number two, you confirm it and move on. If number three trails, you have the months and the pay periods left to close the gap cleanly. Then add one coordination step: check that the cash balance funding, the $24,500 elective deferral with any catch-up, and the profit-sharing pieces are sequenced so you are not tripping a limit.

The plan rewards consistency and punishes improvisation, exactly like a well-run practice. The midyear check is how you keep it on the rewarding side.

Have you set your funded-to-date against the schedule, or only against the calendar?
