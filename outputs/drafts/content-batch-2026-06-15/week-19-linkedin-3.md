5 numbers on 1 page is the midyear check that prevents a December scramble.

Most year-end reviews disappoint because they arrive after the year is written. Run this at the halfway mark instead, when the data is real and the corrections are still cheap.

Pull five numbers, each beside last year's figure:

One, income versus projection. Annualize year-to-date compensation and distributions.

Two, tax pacing. Taxes paid against projected liability, now that the Q2 estimate is in.

Three, retirement progress. Where you stand against the $72,000 contribution limit, plus any cash balance target.

Four, overhead ratio. This year versus last year versus your benchmark.

Five, spending rate. The real share of after-tax income leaving the household.

Then the part that matters: give each line one decision and one date. Adjust the September estimate. Reset retirement pacing through payroll. Chase the overhead drift. A review that ends in awareness changes nothing. A review that ends in named decisions changes the whole second half.

If you blocked one Saturday this month, which of those five numbers are you least sure of right now?
