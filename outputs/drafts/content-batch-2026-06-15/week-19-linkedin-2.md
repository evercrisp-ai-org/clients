$96,000 of margin walked out the door, and the cause was four points of overhead nobody flagged.

Picture a surgeon whose practice collects $2.4M a year. Last year overhead ran 52%. This year it has quietly drifted to 56%. That is four points, and on $2.4M it is roughly $96,000 of margin gone, with no single dramatic line item to point at.

Drift is the hard part. No one approved a $96,000 increase. A supply contract reset, a staffing ratio crept up, a lease renewed higher, and each piece looked too small to chase. Together they rewrote the year.

This is exactly what a midyear look catches. In July, you can still find the cause and act on it while six months remain to recover the margin. By the time the year-end financials are prepared, you are reading a finished story and resolving to "do better next year."

A surgeon reads imaging for what should not be there. Your practice financials deserve the same eye, on a schedule, before the drift hardens into the new normal.

When did you last set this year's overhead ratio next to last year's?
