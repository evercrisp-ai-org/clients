$26K can disappear from a surgeon's plan with no market loss involved.

Your accountant probably defers income every year. That used to be close to free money, back when everyone assumed tax rates would eventually fall. The OBBBA ended that assumption and locked the 37% top rate in place.

So deferral is now a bet, and the bet only pays when next year's rate is genuinely lower than this year's.

For a surgeon projecting $850K this year and a similar number next year, deferring $200K of distributions changes nothing. The dollars are taxed at 37% in both years. You delayed the bill, tied up the cash, and kept the identical result.

The $26K shows up only when the calendar actually differs. Defer that same $200K into a planned step-back year near the 24% bracket, and the timing earns its keep. Defer it out of habit into an identical year, and you gave up flexibility for nothing.

The lever is real. Pulled deliberately, it saves five figures. Pulled on autopilot, it quietly costs you.

When was the last time your income deferral was tied to next year's actual rate instead of last year's routine?
