## Content

### Caption Script

Deferring income is a bet on next year's tax rate.

For most of the last decade that bet was easy, because everyone assumed rates would eventually drift down. The One Big Beautiful Bill Act ended that assumption. The 37% top rate is permanent now.

So if your income next year looks like your income this year, deferral does not lower your rate. It just delays the bill and ties up your cash.

The honest question before you defer anything: is next year actually a lower-rate year, or are you moving money out of habit?
