## Content

### Caption Script

$125,000. That is roughly where a $250,000-a-year cash balance plan should be funded by mid-July.

A cash balance plan is not a flexible 401(k). It is a defined benefit plan, with an actuary setting a required contribution range. The plan expects that range to be funded on schedule.

If you are sitting at $40,000 against a $125,000 target, that gap is real. Caught now, you re-pace it across six months. Caught in December, you are trying to push six figures through payroll in the final weeks, and that often simply cannot happen.

So the question this week is direct: where is your plan against its halfway mark?
