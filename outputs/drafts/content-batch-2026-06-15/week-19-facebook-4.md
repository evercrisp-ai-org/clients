A solo midyear review takes four or five hours, mostly spent gathering numbers. The same review with an advisor who already holds your figures is closer to two hours, and almost all of that time goes to judgment instead of data entry. The work is the same five numbers either way. The difference is how much of your Saturday it costs and how sharp the decisions are at the end.
