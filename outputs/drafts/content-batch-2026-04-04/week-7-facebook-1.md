Whether you file this week or extend, here's one thing worth putting on the calendar before April 15:

A 30-minute call with your financial advisor to set the planning agenda for the next six months.

Not a tax review. Not a portfolio check-in. A planning conversation. What moves should be on the table between now and October? S-Corp salary adjustments? Retirement plan changes? Entity restructuring? Roth conversions?

That one call, before the April 15 rush, is worth more than the last three months of tax preparation. Because it shifts you from reacting to deadlines to building a plan you can execute on your own timeline.

The surgeons who feel most in control of their financial lives aren't the ones with the most complex strategies. They're the ones who have a conversation before the deadline, not after it.
