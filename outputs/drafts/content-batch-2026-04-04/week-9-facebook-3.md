If you're heading to a conference this season and practice transition comes up (it will), here are three questions worth asking in any hallway conversation.

When a colleague says they sold, ask: How long did it take from first conversation to close? Deals that close in four months usually mean the surgeon didn't have time to structure the sale properly. Nine to fourteen months is a healthier timeline.

When someone mentions a PE firm, ask: How did they handle personal goodwill? If they don't know what you mean, that tells you a lot. Personal goodwill allocation can shift the tax bill on a $4 million sale by $300,000 or more.

When the earnout comes up (and it will), ask: What does it look like three years out? The headline multiple sounds great until you learn that 40% of the price depends on hitting performance targets you might not control.

These aren't confrontational questions. They're just the ones that cut through the excitement and get to the structure underneath.

You don't need to be an expert in practice transition to ask them. You just need to be curious enough to listen for what most people leave out.
