Here's a pattern I keep thinking about.

Every April, surgeons go through the tax season ritual. Gather documents, send them to the CPA, review the return, write the check (or set up the extension). And then... silence. Six months of financial quiet until someone taps them on the shoulder in November and says, "We need to figure out your year-end strategy."

That six-month gap is where the most valuable planning happens. Or doesn't.

The surgeons who build wealth most efficiently treat April 16 as the start of something, not the end. They use the spring and summer to recalibrate salaries, establish retirement plans, review entity structures, and model Roth conversions. By the time year-end arrives, they're executing, not scrambling.

Tax season ends next week. Planning season starts the day after.

What's on your calendar for the next six months?
