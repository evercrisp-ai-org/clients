OBBBA made the 37% top marginal rate permanent. That changed the math on Roth conversions more than most people realize.

The traditional advice was straightforward: if you're in a high bracket now and expect to be lower later, convert to Roth. Pay taxes today, enjoy tax-free growth tomorrow.

But at 37% federal plus your state rate, you may be converting at a combined 42% or higher. If you retire in a lower-tax or no-tax state and withdraw at an effective rate of 22% to 24%, the math on the conversion depends heavily on the growth period and the amount.

For small conversions in years where your income dips (say, a quarter with lower surgical volume), the numbers can work beautifully. For large conversions at peak earning years, the analysis often favors keeping the money in a traditional IRA and paying lower rates in retirement.

The right approach isn't reflexive. It's analytical. And the analysis requires real income data, which is why September 30 is the deadline for running the numbers with enough time to execute before December 31.

If your Roth strategy hasn't been re-evaluated since the 37% rate became permanent, this is the quarter to revisit it.
