April 15 is next week. You're filing or extending. What happens after will matter more than either of those decisions.

Most surgeons mentally close the financial chapter after April 15. The return gets filed. The CPA goes quiet. And for the next six months, nothing happens.

That gap is expensive. At surgeon income levels, four specific planning moves with deadlines between May and September can capture $35,000 to $60,000 in annual tax-advantaged savings:

S-Corp salary recalibration (May 15). Cash balance plan establishment (July 1). Entity restructuring evaluation (August 31). Roth conversion analysis (September 30).

Each has a window. Each has a deadline. And each one closes whether you act or not.

The surgeons who build wealth most efficiently don't have better CPAs. They have a protocol for the six months that everyone else wastes.

What's your post-tax-season plan?
