The best time to review your practice's entity structure was three years ago. The second best time is this spring.

Practices evolve. Revenue changes. Partners come and go. Ancillary revenue streams emerge. But the S-Corp or LLC that was set up when the practice launched tends to stay exactly as it was, regardless of how much the practice has changed around it.

If your practice looks meaningfully different than it did three years ago (new associates, ASC involvement, revenue shifts, transition on the horizon), it's worth spending an hour this spring evaluating whether your entity still fits.

Entity changes take time to implement. Legal documents, tax elections, operating agreements. All of it needs to be in place before January 1 to be effective. Starting the evaluation now gives you the runway to make thoughtful decisions instead of rushed ones.

Not everything needs to change. But everything should be looked at.
