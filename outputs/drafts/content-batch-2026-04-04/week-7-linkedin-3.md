More than half of high-income taxpayers file extensions every year. The reason matters less than what they do with the time.

Filing an extension gives you until October 15. That's six months. The question is whether you use those six months for optimization or just for procrastination.

The surgeons who use the extension window strategically do four things before October:

They recalibrate their S-Corp salary against current-year revenue (by May 15). They evaluate whether a cash balance plan should be established or amended (by July 1). They review their entity structure for alignment with current goals (by August 31). They run a Roth conversion analysis with real income data (by September 30).

None of these require the return to be filed. All of them require the kind of intentional planning that a six-month runway makes possible.

If you filed an extension this week, you didn't delay. You created a planning window. Use it.
