## Content

### Caption Script

If you want a cash balance plan for 2026, the clock starts April 16. Here's why July 1 matters.

A cash balance plan allows annual tax-deferred contributions of $150,000 to $350,000, depending on your age and compensation. That's on top of your 401(k). For surgeons over 50 earning $700K or more, it's the single highest-leverage tax-sheltered savings vehicle available.

But establishing a new plan requires a plan document drafted by an ERISA attorney, actuarial certification, and IRS compliance setup. That process takes 6 to 10 weeks.

Start in April or May? You get a July 1 effective date with half-year funding of $75,000 to $150,000.

Start in September? The actuaries are backed up. The window has closed.

$75,000 to $150,000 in tax-deferred savings. Gone. Because the conversation happened four months too late.
