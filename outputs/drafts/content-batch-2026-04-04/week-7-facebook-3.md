Quick thought for anyone who owns a practice and is over 50.

The most you can contribute to a 401(k) in 2026 is $32,500 (including catch-up). For a surgeon earning $700K or more, that's a fraction of what's available.

A cash balance plan, layered on top of the 401(k), allows annual tax-deferred contributions of $150,000 to $350,000 depending on age and plan design. Same practice. Same income. Dramatically different savings capacity.

The catch? Establishing a new plan takes 6 to 10 weeks. If you start this spring, you can have it in place by July with meaningful first-year funding. Wait until fall, and this year's window closes.

Not the kind of thing to rush into. But it is the kind of thing worth asking about now rather than later.
