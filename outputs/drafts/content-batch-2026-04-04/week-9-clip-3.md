## Content

### Caption Script

They'll call it a partnership. Here's how to tell if it actually is one.

Four signals.

Compensation trajectory. In a real partnership, your total compensation (clinical plus equity) is projected to increase over the hold period. If clinical comp drops and the only upside is locked in illiquid rollover equity, that's a red flag. Ask for the year-by-year projection.

Clinical autonomy. Do you still choose your cases, your implants, your schedule? Or does the platform start making those decisions based on preferred vendor contracts and utilization targets? Get the specifics in writing.

Governance. Do you have a vote on hiring, expansion, and capital decisions? Or are you consulted but not empowered? There's a big difference between a seat at the table and a chair in the hallway.

Second-bite economics. The "second bite of the apple" is where the biggest upside lives. In a genuine partnership, you know the platform's current valuation, the projected exit timeline, and your share. If the projections are vague and the management team takes a disproportionate share through carried interest, that's extraction with a handshake.

Four signals. Four honest conversations before you sign anything.
