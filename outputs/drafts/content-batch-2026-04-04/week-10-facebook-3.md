Six words that should make any practice-owning surgeon pause: "My practice IS my retirement plan."

I understand why so many surgeons feel this way. The practice generates significant income. It has value. It feels like everything you need financially is wrapped up in this one thing you've built.

But a retirement plan does specific things. It compounds through reinvestment. It spreads risk across multiple asset classes. It protects against scenarios like disability or market shifts. And at a specific point in the future, it converts accumulated value into reliable income you can live on.

Your practice does almost none of those things on its own.

The practice doesn't compound automatically. It doesn't diversify your risk (in fact, it concentrates it). If you can't operate for a year, the practice value declines. And converting practice value into actual retirement income requires a complex, multi-year process with no guaranteed outcome.

Your practice can be a significant part of your retirement plan. Absolutely. But when it IS the entire plan, you're carrying concentrated risk in a single asset without the protections that actual retirement structures are designed to provide.

The fix isn't dramatic. It starts with building financial structures alongside the practice: retirement accounts that compound independently, savings that diversify your wealth, and a timeline that gives you options before you need them.

One word changes everything. From "IS" to "part of."
