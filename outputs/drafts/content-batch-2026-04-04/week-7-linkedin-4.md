Consider a surgeon who launched her S-Corp eight years ago, when she was the sole owner producing $600K in annual revenue. Today, the practice has two associates, an ASC ownership stake, and revenue north of $1.8M. The entity structure? Still the same single S-Corp from 2018.

That's not unusual. Practice structures tend to be "set and forget" items. The CPA files the return. The attorney reviews the operating agreement when asked. Nobody proactively evaluates whether the entity still fits.

But practices evolve. Revenue shifts. Partners join. Ancillary revenue streams emerge. And the entity structure that was optimal for a solo practitioner generating $600K may be leaving significant money on the table for a multi-provider, multi-entity operation at $1.8M.

Common triggers for a restructuring evaluation: adding a partner or associate, creating an ASC ownership stake, revenue growth or decline beyond 30%, or being within five years of a potential practice transition.

If any of those apply to you, the evaluation window is now. Entity restructuring requires legal documents, tax elections, and operating agreement updates, all of which must be complete before January 1 to be effective for the next tax year. August 31 is the latest practical start date for a clean implementation.

When was the last time your entity structure was reviewed?
