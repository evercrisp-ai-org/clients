## Content

### Caption Script

Four financial moves, four deadlines, six months. Here's the post-tax-season protocol.

Move one: S-Corp salary recalibration. Deadline, May 15. Your reasonable compensation was probably set years ago. Adjusting it by $50,000 can unlock $20,000 or more in additional retirement plan contributions. One conversation with your payroll provider.

Move two: cash balance plan establishment. Deadline, July 1. If you're over 50 and earning $700K or more, this vehicle shelters $150,000 to $350,000 per year on top of your 401(k). But the setup takes 6 to 10 weeks, so starting now gives you a July 1 effective date with meaningful first-year funding.

Move three: entity restructuring evaluation. Deadline, August 31. If your practice structure hasn't been reviewed in three or more years, this is the window. Legal documents, tax elections, and operating agreements all need time to be ready before January 1.

Move four: Roth conversion analysis. Deadline, September 30. With OBBBA making the 37% rate permanent, the conversion math has shifted. Run the analysis in the summer when you have enough income data to model accurately but enough time to execute before December 31.

Total first-year value: $35,000 to $60,000 in tax-advantaged savings. The window opens April 16.
