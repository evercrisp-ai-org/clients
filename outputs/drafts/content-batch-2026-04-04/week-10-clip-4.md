## Content

### Caption Script

Consider a surgeon looking at a PE pitch deck. It's beautiful. The slides are polished. The multiple is compelling. The word "partnership" is on every page. It's also missing four things.

First: rollover equity. Most PE deals require you to reinvest 30% to 35% of the sale price back into the acquiring entity. On a $4M deal, that's $1.4 million that stays illiquid, in someone else's control, for another 3 to 5 years.

Second: compensation adjustments. Post-acquisition, surgeon compensation often declines 15% to 25% through productivity thresholds, management fees, and staffing changes. The Year 1 guarantee is not the long-term number.

Third: EBITDA normalization. How the buyer calculates your earnings directly affects your valuation and your earn-out targets. If they've normalized your EBITDA upward to make the offer look generous, the performance targets may be unrealistic.

Fourth: the hold period. For 3 to 5 years after closing, you're an employee of the entity that bought your practice. New reporting. New compensation formulas. Reduced autonomy.

None of this means the deal is bad. But it means the pitch deck is incomplete. And conference season is exactly the time to know what's not on the slides.
