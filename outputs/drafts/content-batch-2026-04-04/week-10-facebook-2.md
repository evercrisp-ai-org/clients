Here's a number most practice-owning surgeons have never calculated: what percentage of your total net worth is sitting in your practice?

Not your revenue. Not your collections. The actual value of your practice as a percentage of everything you own.

For many orthopedic surgeons, the answer is somewhere between 40% and 55%. When you add up the practice valuation, your home equity, your investment and retirement accounts, and everything else, the practice often represents the single largest piece of the pie. By a wide margin.

You'd never put 46% of your investment portfolio in one stock. Especially one you can't sell for years, can't hedge, and whose value depends entirely on your personal ability to keep working.

But that's what practice ownership often looks like when you zoom out and see the full picture.

Knowing this number doesn't mean you need to sell tomorrow. It means you need to treat your practice with the same analytical rigor you'd apply to any asset of its size. Calculate the concentration. Understand the risk. And make sure your financial plan accounts for both.

It takes about 15 minutes to run the numbers. Worth doing this week.
