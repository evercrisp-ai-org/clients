$130,000 to $350,000. That's the tax difference between documenting your personal goodwill before a practice sale and not documenting it at all.

Personal goodwill is the value of everything that walks out the door when you leave: your surgical reputation, your referral network, your patient relationships. When properly documented, that value is taxed at long-term capital gains rates (23.8%). Without documentation, it can be taxed at ordinary income rates approaching 40.8%.

On a $4 million practice sale, the allocation between personal and enterprise goodwill determines whether your tax bill is $950,000 or $1,280,000. Same sale price. Same buyer. Same practice. The only variable is documentation that must exist before negotiations begin.

The IRS scrutinizes after-the-fact personal goodwill claims. Courts have reduced allocations from 75% to 15% when the documentation appeared to be manufactured for a pending transaction.

Three components to a defensible claim: a non-compete agreement (2+ years before any sale), a referral source analysis (24 months of data), and a formal personal goodwill appraisal.

If you're 3-5 years from a potential transition, this is the most valuable tax conversation to have this quarter.
