## Content

### Caption Script

Who are you when you're not a surgeon?

I know that question sounds philosophical. Maybe even uncomfortable. But it is the single most important question in any practice transition.

A surgeon who has been operating for 20 or 30 years has built an identity that goes far deeper than a job title. You wake up with a purpose. You walk into a room where your judgment, your skill, your decisions matter in ways most professions never approach.

When that identity goes quiet, something fills the space. And if you haven't done the work to decide what that something is, the default is spending. A country club membership to have somewhere to go. A speculative investment to feel productive. A lifestyle upgrade to replace the stimulation the OR used to provide.

That question, "who are you when you're not a surgeon?", is worth more than any valuation.
