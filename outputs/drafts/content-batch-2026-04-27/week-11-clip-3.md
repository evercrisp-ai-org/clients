## Content

### Caption Script

Three things you need to document now if you plan to sell your practice in the next five years.

First: a non-compete agreement between you and your practice entity. This establishes that you have individual value to the practice. It needs to be in place at least two years before any buyer conversations. Timing is the most scrutinized element in personal goodwill disputes.

Second: a referral source analysis. Track where your patients come from. Specifically, how many arrive through relationships tied to you personally versus through the practice's institutional channels. You need at least 24 months of data to establish a credible pattern.

Third: a personal goodwill appraisal. A formal valuation that separates total practice goodwill into personal and enterprise components. Completed at least 18 months before any anticipated sale.

Total documentation timeline: 12 to 24 months. The cost is measured in thousands. The savings are measured in hundreds of thousands. The clock already started.
