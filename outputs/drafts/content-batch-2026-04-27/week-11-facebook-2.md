Something worth thinking about: every referral relationship you've built over 20 or 25 years has a dollar value. Every patient who chose your practice because of your name. Every colleague who sends cases your way because they trust your clinical judgment.

That collective value is called personal goodwill. And when you eventually sell or transition your practice, how that value is classified for tax purposes can mean the difference of $130,000 to $350,000 in what you keep.

The surprising part? Most surgeons learn about this distinction after the sale process has started. By then, the documentation window has closed.

If you're three to five years from any kind of practice transition, this is one of those conversations worth having early. The documentation is straightforward. The timeline is the part that matters.
