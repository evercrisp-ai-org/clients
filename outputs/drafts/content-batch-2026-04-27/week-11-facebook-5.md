Your surgical reputation, your referral network, your patient relationships. You built all of that over decades of practice. It represents something real and valuable.

When the time comes to transition your practice, making sure that value is properly documented and properly classified for tax purposes could be the difference between keeping an extra $130,000 to $350,000 and losing it to a tax bill that didn't have to be that high.

This isn't about aggressive tax strategy. It's about making sure the value you created is recognized and treated fairly. The documentation is straightforward. The key is starting early enough for it to be credible.

If you have colleagues who are thinking about practice transitions in the next few years, this might be worth sharing.
