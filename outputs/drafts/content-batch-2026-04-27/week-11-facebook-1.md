Here's a number worth knowing if you're anywhere near a practice transition in the next five years: $330,000.

That's the potential tax difference between documenting your personal goodwill before a practice sale and not documenting it at all.

Personal goodwill is the value of your reputation, your referral relationships, and your patient loyalty. When documented properly, it's taxed at capital gains rates. Without documentation, it can be taxed at ordinary income rates.

Same practice. Same sale price. Same buyer. The only difference is preparation that takes 12 to 24 months to build.

If this is relevant to your situation, the most valuable thing you can do this month is ask your financial advisor about personal goodwill documentation.
