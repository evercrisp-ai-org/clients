## Content

### Caption Script

23.8% or 40.8%. The tax rate on your practice sale depends entirely on one thing: whether you documented your personal goodwill before the sale process began.

Personal goodwill is your surgical reputation, your referral relationships, the patients who come to your practice because of your name. When documented properly, that value is taxed as a long-term capital gain at 23.8%.

Without documentation? The same value can be taxed at ordinary income rates approaching 40.8%.

On a $4 million practice sale, the difference is $130,000 to $350,000. And the documentation takes 12 to 24 months to build. If you plan to sell in the next five years, you should have started two years ago. The next best time is now.
