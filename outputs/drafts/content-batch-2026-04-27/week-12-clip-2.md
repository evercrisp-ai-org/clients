## Content

### Caption Script

If your spouse is on your practice payroll, a buyer is already adjusting for it. Here's what that means for your valuation.

Consider a practice where the surgeon's spouse handles billing and some office management at $120,000 per year. The market rate for that role is approximately $55,000. The difference, $65,000, gets added back to the practice's normalized EBITDA.

A buyer doesn't price the relationship. They price the role. If that role costs $55,000 on the open market, that's the number in the buyer's financial model.

Now multiply that $65,000 adjustment by a 4x valuation multiple. That single line item in your payroll records is worth $260,000 in practice valuation.

Family employment in a practice is common and serves legitimate purposes: income splitting, benefits coverage, genuine work contribution. Normalization doesn't make it wrong. It just means a buyer measures it differently than you do.

Knowing this adjustment exists, and understanding how it affects your valuation, is the difference between being surprised by a buyer's analysis and presenting your own.
