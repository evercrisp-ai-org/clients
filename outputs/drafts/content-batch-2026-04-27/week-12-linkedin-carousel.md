## Slide outline

1. **Title:** "$5.5M or $4.4M?" — sub: "Five adjustments a buyer makes to your practice financials"
2. **What is normalized EBITDA?** Your earnings adjusted for anything specific to you as the current owner. Buyers use this number, not your reported net income.
3. **Adjustment 1: Owner Compensation.** Your salary vs. market rate. The excess gets added back. Example: $800K salary, $650K market = $150K add-back.
4. **Adjustment 2: Family Employment.** The role gets priced at market, regardless of the relationship. Example: $120K payroll, $55K market = $65K add-back.
5. **Adjustment 3: Personal Expenses.** Vehicle leases, memberships, personal travel. If a buyer wouldn't pay it, it gets added back. Example: $21.6K.
6. **Adjustment 4: One-Time Costs.** Legal disputes, unusual repairs, equipment write-offs. Remove what won't recur. Example: $45K.
7. **Adjustment 5: Lease Terms.** When you own the building and lease below market, the buyer adjusts. Goes both directions.
8. **The math:** $1.1M + $281.6K in adjustments = $1.38M normalized EBITDA. At 4x: $5.5M vs. $4.4M.
9. **Close:** "Which adjustment would move your number the most?" (mirrors Thursday mechanism post)
