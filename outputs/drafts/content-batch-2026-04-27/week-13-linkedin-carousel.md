## Slide outline

1. **Title:** "Three Elements of a Next-Chapter Plan" — sub: "Identity work, purpose architecture, financial guardrails"
2. **The Problem:** Surgeons who exit without a plan lose 30-40% of transition wealth in three years. The cause is not bad investing. It is unstructured decisions filling an identity gap.
3. **Element 1 — Identity Work:** "Who are you when you're not a surgeon?" Start 12-24 months before the transition. Reflect on what you'll miss, what you'll release, and what activities could replace the stimulation the OR provides.
4. **Element 2 — Purpose Architecture:** Design what your time, energy, and expertise will be directed toward. Board positions, teaching, mentoring, clinical consulting, advisory roles. Purpose is built, not found.
5. **Element 3 — Financial Guardrails:** A defined budget before the sale. A systematic investment plan before the money arrives. A 90-day waiting period for any large deployment. Quarterly reviews for the first two years.
6. **The Math:** Same $2.9M net. With a plan: $3.2M by year three. Without: $1.7M. The $1.5M difference is structure.
7. **Close:** "The deal determines how much wealth you create. Your next-chapter plan determines whether it lasts." (mirror text post)
