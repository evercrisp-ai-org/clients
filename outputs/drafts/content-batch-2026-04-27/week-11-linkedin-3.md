$400,000 in personal goodwill was reduced to $60,000 in one case because the documentation arrived at the same time as the deal.

The IRS evaluates personal goodwill claims on three criteria. Timing: was the documentation created before acquisition discussions began? Substance: does the documentation reflect a genuine, pre-existing economic reality? Reasonableness: is the allocation proportional to the surgeon's actual role in driving practice value?

Courts have been consistent. Documentation created during or immediately before a sale negotiation is treated as a tax position, not an economic reality. The reduction in that case from 75% to 15% allocation turned a $400,000 tax benefit into roughly $60,000.

The defensible approach has three steps. First, execute a non-compete agreement between you and your practice entity at least 24 months before any anticipated sale. Second, begin systematic referral source tracking that produces two or more years of data. Third, commission a formal personal goodwill appraisal using the "with and without" methodology.

Each component takes time to build credibility. None requires a buyer to be identified. All can be done alongside normal practice operations.

The documentation window is measured in years. If you're within five years of a potential exit, start this quarter.
