## Content

### Caption Script

Two surgeons. Same exit value. Both netted $2.9 million after taxes. Here's where they stood three years later.

Surgeon A: no next-chapter plan. Year one, lifestyle expanded by 30%, spending hit $455,000. A $500,000 speculative investment with a friend. Year two, another $200,000 in drawdowns. The investment produced nothing. Year three balance: $1.7 million. A 41% decline.

Surgeon B: she built a plan 18 months before the sale. A structured annual budget of $375,000. A diversified investment plan designed before the money arrived. A 90-day waiting period for any purchase or investment over $100,000. And identity work: board positions, a teaching role, a mentorship commitment.

Year one: the budget held. The portfolio was deployed systematically. Year two: steady withdrawals, regular rebalancing, purpose-driven activities filling the days. Year three balance: $3.2 million.

$1.5 million apart. Same starting line. The difference was not investment returns. The difference was structure: identity work, purpose architecture, and financial guardrails.

The next chapter is a plan, not a hope.
