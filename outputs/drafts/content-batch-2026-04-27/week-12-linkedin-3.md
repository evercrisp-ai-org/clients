$5.5 million or $4.4 million. Five adjustments in 30 minutes separate the two numbers.

A buyer doesn't use your reported net income to value your practice. They use normalized EBITDA: your earnings adjusted for anything specific to you as the current owner. Here are the five adjustments and what they look like on a practice with $1.1M in reported net income.

Owner compensation above market: $150K add-back. Your $800K salary vs. $650K market rate for a non-owner surgeon. Family employment above market: $65K add-back. A $120K role that costs $55K on the open market. Personal expenses through the business: $21.6K add-back. Vehicle leases, memberships, personal travel. One-time, non-recurring costs: $45K add-back. Legal disputes, unusual repairs, equipment write-offs. Below-market lease terms: varies. Common when the surgeon owns the building.

Total adjustments: $281,600. Normalized EBITDA: $1.38M. At a 4x multiple, the valuation moves from $4.4M to $5.5M.

The framework takes 30 minutes with your most recent P&L. Start with net income, add back ITDA, calculate your comp above market, list personal expenses, identify one-time costs. Write it down. That number is closer to what a buyer would calculate than anything on your tax return.

Which adjustment would move your number the most?
