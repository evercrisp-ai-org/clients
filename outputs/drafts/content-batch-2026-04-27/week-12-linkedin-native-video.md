## On-camera script (~60 seconds)

"$1.1 million is the illustrated gap between what your reported net income says your practice is worth and what a buyer would actually calculate.

The difference comes from five adjustments a buyer makes to your financials. They strip out your above-market compensation, reprice any family members on payroll to market rates, remove personal expenses running through the business, add back one-time costs, and adjust lease terms between related parties.

Those five adjustments, on a practice with $1.1 million in reported net income, can push normalized EBITDA to $1.38 million. At a 4x multiple, the valuation moves from $4.4 million to $5.5 million.

You can calculate a rough version of this number in about 30 minutes with your most recent P&L. Start with net income, add back interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, then work through the five adjustments.

Write the number down. Compare it to the number you've been carrying in your head.

If there's a gap, you now know what a buyer already knows."
