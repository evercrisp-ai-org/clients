## Content

### Caption Script

A surgeon netted $2.9 million from a practice sale. Good deal. Strong negotiation. Solid tax planning.

Three years later, $1.7 million was left. A 41% decline. Here's what happened.

Year one: lifestyle expanded by about 30%. Annual spend jumped to $455,000. At the same time, $500,000 went into a speculative investment with a friend. That's nearly a million dollars deployed in year one with no framework behind it.

Year two: another $200,000 in drawdowns. The investment from year one produced nothing. The elevated spending felt normal.

Year three: $1.7 million. No single catastrophe. No market crash. No fraud. Just the absence of a plan.

The spending filled an identity gap. The investments filled a productivity gap. And neither had guardrails.
