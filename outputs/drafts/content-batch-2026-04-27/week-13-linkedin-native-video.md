## On-camera script (~60 seconds)

"$2.9 million in net proceeds from a practice sale looks like the finish line. For most surgeons, it is the starting line of a much harder question: who are you when you're not operating?

That question drives more financial outcomes than any deal term. Surgeons who exit without answering it tend to fill the identity gap with spending. Lifestyle up 30%. A speculative investment that feels like productivity. Drawdowns that compound because the elevated spending feels earned.

The alternative is a next-chapter plan with three elements. First, identity work: honest reflection on who you are beyond the OR, started 12 to 18 months before the transition. Second, purpose architecture: activities that provide intellectual engagement, social connection, and contribution, designed before the sale closes. Third, financial guardrails: a defined budget, a systematic investment plan, and a waiting period for any large deployment.

The deal determines how much wealth you create. Your next-chapter plan determines whether it lasts."
