## Content

When 3 infrastructure gaps compound inside an independent practice, the RVU differential widens and the valuation discount follows. Here is the diagnostic.

Hospital-employed orthopedists lead on work RVUs for three compounding reasons: protected OR time that eliminates scheduling volatility, case coordinators who own patient flow and pre-op prep, and referral systems that route volume without business development overhead.

Each of these is improvable in an independent practice.

At a 4x multiple, a 10 to 15 percent improvement in annual work RVU output is worth $500,000 to $600,000 in additional practice value. The return comes from operational improvements in scheduling, coordination, and referral processes. The clinical hours stay the same.

Three questions to work through this week:

How does your annual work RVU output compare to the MGMA benchmark for your subspecialty and employment model?

What percentage of your clinical week goes to administrative work that could be systematized or delegated?

If you are 3 to 5 years from a transaction, is your productivity data building a case for an independence premium?

Your billing system and practice manager can pull the first number in 48 hours. That is where the analysis starts.

What does that comparison tell you about where the operational work is?
