## Clip Script

[HOOK — first 3 seconds]
Hospital-employed orthopedic surgeons now generate more work RVUs per year than their independent counterparts. That's a first. The driver is infrastructure, and it changes how buyers value your practice.

[CORE CONTENT — 35-45 seconds]
For the last several years, MGMA and AAOS compensation surveys showed independent practice owners leading on productivity. The most recent data cycle reversed that. Hospital-employed orthopedists now outperform on median work RVUs by roughly 10 to 15 percent.

What drove the shift? Infrastructure. Hospital systems have invested in scheduling support, protected OR time, and case coordination. An employed surgeon shows up and operates. An independent practice owner operates, and also manages the schedule, the billing disputes, and the administrative overhead that comes with running your own business.

The CMS efficiency adjustment that has been cutting reimbursement on non-time-based procedures since January compounds this. Independent practices absorbed both a revenue headwind and an administrative burden that hospital systems largely shielded their physicians from.

[CLOSING BEAT — 5-8 seconds]
The productivity gap is real. What it means for your practice's exit value is what the full episode covers.
