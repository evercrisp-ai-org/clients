## Content

10 to 15 percent more work RVUs. That's how far hospital-employed orthopedists have pulled ahead of independent practice owners on the most recent MGMA productivity benchmarks.

This is a first.

For years, the conventional assumption was that independent practice owners outworked their employed counterparts. You controlled your schedule, built your own referral base, and generated more volume. The data confirmed it.

The most recent benchmark cycle reversed that assumption.

The driver is structural, and it is worth understanding precisely. Hospital systems have built scheduling infrastructure that lets their physicians operate more. Protected OR time. Dedicated case coordinators. Referral channeling. An employed surgeon shows up and operates. An independent surgeon operates and runs the business.

The CMS efficiency cut to non-time-based procedures, in effect since January, compounded the gap on the revenue side while independent practices absorbed the administrative overhead that hospital systems largely insulated their physicians from.

What this data means for your practice's valuation is different from what it means for your productivity. Buyers use hospital benchmarks to stress-test forward revenue. A practice that outperforms the benchmark holds its value. A practice that tracks below it invites a discount.

What does your RVU output tell a buyer about your practice right now?
