## Content

The hospital RVU advantage over independent practice owners sits almost entirely in three operational categories.

Protected OR time. Case coordination. Referral process design.

Hospital systems built these out over the past several years, which let their physicians spend more time operating and less time managing the infrastructure around surgery. The result shows up in the MGMA benchmark data: hospital-employed orthopedists now lead independent practice owners on median work RVU output.

Here's what this means practically: each of those three areas is improvable in an independent practice. At a 4x multiple, a 10 to 15 percent improvement in annual work RVU output translates to $500,000 to $600,000 in additional practice value.

The operational work that closes the RVU gap is the same work that protects the practice's valuation. If you're in the 3-to-10-year window before a transition, this is the kind of analysis worth sitting with.
