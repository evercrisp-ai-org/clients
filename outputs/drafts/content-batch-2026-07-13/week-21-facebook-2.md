## Content

$560,000. That's the gap in exit value between a practice that has adapted to the new RVU benchmark and one that hasn't.

Consider a practice with $1.45 million in normalized EBITDA, worth $5.8 million at a 4x multiple. When a buyer models forward revenue using the hospital productivity benchmark rather than the selling surgeon's personal output, a 10 percent revenue adjustment drops the EBITDA to $1.31 million. At 4x, the practice is worth $5.24 million.

The gap appears before a single term is negotiated. It comes from a market benchmark that moved and a practice that hadn't yet responded to it.

Hospital-employed orthopedists now generate more work RVUs per year than independent practice owners, and sophisticated buyers are using that data in their acquisition models.

Knowing where your output sits relative to the benchmark is the starting point.
