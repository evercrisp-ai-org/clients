## Clip Script

[HOOK — first 3 seconds]
Private equity firms in orthopedics are using the RVU benchmark data actively to price deals. Here's what that means for you.

[CORE CONTENT — 30-38 seconds]
When a PE firm evaluates a practice, they build an acquisition model and an exit model. Both depend heavily on physician productivity assumptions. And right now, the benchmark they're using is the hospital productivity data.

A surgeon who outperforms the hospital benchmark on work RVUs is a better acquisition target. It signals productivity will hold or improve post-close as administrative burden decreases. PE investors price that confidence with a better offer.

A surgeon who underperforms introduces a recovery timeline. The PE firm assumes they'll close the productivity gap over the hold period, and they price in the cost of that recovery by offering less today.

[CLOSING BEAT — 5-8 seconds]
The analysis has already been run on your practice. The question is whether you've run it first.
