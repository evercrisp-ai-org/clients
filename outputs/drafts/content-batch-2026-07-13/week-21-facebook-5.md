## Content

There's a reason most orthopedic surgeons who chose independence don't regret it.

The autonomy to make clinical decisions without a system algorithm weighing in. The ownership of something you built from the ground up. The compounding value of a referral network that reflects two decades of earned trust.

That value is real. And it's documentable at capital gains rates when properly structured.

The RVU benchmark data is diagnostic information. It tells you where a market shift has created a gap in how buyers model your practice's forward value. Addressing that gap does not require a different practice model. It requires the same precision you bring to the OR: current data, an honest read of where things stand, and a plan calibrated to what's actually happening now.

The productivity benchmark shifted. The surgeons who act on that information before a buyer uses it first are the ones who exit on terms they chose.
