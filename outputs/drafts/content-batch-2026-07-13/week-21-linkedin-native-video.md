## Video Script

**[OPENING — 0:00-0:10]**

Hospital-employed orthopedic surgeons now generate more work RVUs per year than independent practice owners. That's a first, and it carries a direct financial implication for your practice's value.

**[CORE — 0:10-0:48]**

The shift is entirely infrastructure-driven. Hospital systems protect OR time, staff coordinators to manage patient flow, and route referrals directly. An employed surgeon shows up and operates. An independent practice owner operates and runs the business.

The CMS efficiency adjustment, active since January, compounded this: it cut reimbursement on non-time-based procedures while independent practices absorbed the administrative overhead that hospital systems largely offset.

Here is what that means for exit value. A practice with $1.45 million in normalized EBITDA is worth $5.8 million at a 4x multiple. If a buyer stress-tests the forward revenue using the hospital benchmark, and applies a 10 percent productivity adjustment, the practice comes in at $5.24 million.

$560,000 moved out of the exit outcome before a single term was negotiated.

**[CLOSE — 0:48-1:05]**

Pull your work RVU output against the MGMA benchmark for your subspecialty. That number tells you whether you have an independence premium or a gap to close.

If you want to walk through what this means for your own situation, the link to reach me is in my profile.
