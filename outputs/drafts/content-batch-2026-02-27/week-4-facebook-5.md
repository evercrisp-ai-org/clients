This month we've been talking about the number that matters more than collections: your effective hourly rate. What you actually keep for every hour of clinical work after overhead, taxes, retirement, and debt service flow through.

In April, you'll get to see the other side of that coin.

Whether you file your return or extend, the data in that filing tells a story. It shows you exactly how your current structure performed over the last twelve months. How much went to taxes. How much was sheltered. Whether the entity, the retirement plan, and the salary were all working together or pulling in different directions.

Your effective hourly rate and your tax return are two views of the same picture. One shows you the result per hour. The other shows you the structure that produced it.

When that return is in front of you next month, you'll have everything you need to see clearly whether your financial structure is doing what it should. We'll be digging into exactly that in April.
